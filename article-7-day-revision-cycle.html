7-Day Revision Cycle for Quant

This weekly revision loop is designed for government exam aspirants who want steady quant improvement without mental overload. Instead of random topic jumping, this structure keeps your memory fresh, speed stable, and confidence high.

Day 1: Table Recall and Reverse Logic

Revise tables, reverse products, and quick division mapping. Keep this fast and timed. Goal is reflex-level recall, not written solving.

Day 2: Arithmetic Speed Drill

Practice mixed multiplication patterns and short simplification sets. Track time per question and error type.

Day 3: Squares and Cubes

Memorize and test squares to 30 and cubes to 20. Use both direct and reverse question formats.

Day 4: Product Pattern Consolidation

Use consecutive products and factor recognition practice. This improves approximation and mental math for DI and arithmetic.

Day 5: Timed Mini Mock

Attempt one strict timed set. Focus on selection quality, not maximum attempts. Keep a negative-marking safe approach.

Day 6: Weak-Area Repair

Review your wrong notebook and re-solve repeated patterns. Correct method and speed issues separately.

Day 7: Full Simulation and Review

Take a full practice test and do complete analysis. End day with a short summary: three strengths, three corrections for next week.

How to Repeat

Run this cycle for 4 to 6 weeks. Maintain consistency and compare weekly trends in accuracy and time. This method builds long-term exam readiness.

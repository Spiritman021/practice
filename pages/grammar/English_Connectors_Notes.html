💡 How to Use This Reference
  • Sentence-level vs Clause-level: Some connectors (moreover, however) connect full sentences; others (although, unless) connect clauses within one sentence.
  • Formal vs Informal: Notwithstanding, albeit, conversely = formal/written. On top of that, what's more = informal/spoken.
  • Punctuation rule: When a connector starts a sentence, follow with a comma: "However, she succeeded."
  • Mid-sentence connectors: Use semicolons before: "He tried hard; nevertheless, he failed."
  • IBPS/SSC tip: Questions often test whether you choose the correct connector for the relationship — cause, contrast, or addition.
  • Avoid overuse: In essays, vary your connectors. Don't use "however" 5 times in one paragraph.

Adding Information
21 connectors — इसके अतिरिक्त / अतिरिक्त जानकारी

Use these when you want to add more points, examples, or facts to support your argument. They signal: "there is more to say!"

Moreover
इसके अतिरिक्त
Adds a stronger, more important piece of information to what was already said. Often used in formal writing.
The project is not only ahead of schedule, but moreover, it is also under budget.
Extra: More emphatic than "also". Usually placed at the start of a new sentence.
Furthermore
आगे / इसके अतिरिक्त
Adds more information or evidence; slightly more formal than "moreover". Used in essays and reports.
The research shows positive trends; furthermore, it suggests growth opportunities.
Tip: Both "moreover" and "furthermore" can open a sentence. "Furthermore" feels slightly less emphatic than "moreover".
In addition
इसके अलावा
Introduces supplementary information. Works between sentences or as a sentence opener.
The report highlights cost-saving measures. In addition, it suggests areas for further optimization.
Note: "In addition to [noun/gerund]" — e.g., In addition to running, she also swims.
In fact
वास्तव में
Introduces a fact that confirms or reinforces the preceding statement, often with a surprising or emphasizing effect.
He said the task was easy. In fact, it took us three full days to complete it.
Usage: Can also be used to correct a misconception: "It's not cold — in fact, it's quite warm."
For instance / For example
उदाहरण के लिए
Introduces a specific example to illustrate a general point.
Many fruits are rich in vitamins. For instance, oranges are an excellent source of Vitamin C.
Note: "For instance" and "for example" are interchangeable. "e.g." is the written abbreviation.
As a matter of fact
वास्तव में / असल में
Introduces a fact that supports or elaborates on a previous statement. Slightly more colloquial than "in fact".
You think he's new? As a matter of fact, he has been working here for over ten years.
Also / And
भी / और
"Also" adds another fact; "And" connects words, phrases, or clauses. Both are common but should not be overused in formal writing.
She is hardworking and talented. She also volunteers on weekends.
Tip: In formal essays, prefer "moreover" or "furthermore" over starting sentences with "also".
As well as
साथ ही / के साथ-साथ
Used to add an extra item, person, or idea to what has already been mentioned.
The project includes cost analysis as well as market research to ensure its success.
Grammar: "As well as" is NOT the same as "and" for subject-verb agreement — "She, as well as her team, IS working hard."
Besides
अलावा / इसके अतिरिक्त
Adds another point to what has already been mentioned. Can mean "in addition to" or "other than".
Besides his regular job, he also volunteers at a local animal shelter.
On top of that
उसके ऊपर / उसके साथ ही
Informal phrase to add information, often emphasizing something positive or negative. Conversational tone.
She's an excellent student, and on top of that, she's a talented athlete.
Register: Informal/spoken. Avoid in formal essays.
Not only… but also
केवल इतना ही नहीं, बल्कि यह भी
Emphasizes that two related things are both true. Creates a stronger effect than simply listing two points.
The restaurant not only offers delicious food but also provides a cozy atmosphere.
Grammar: Inversion possible — "Not only did he arrive late, but he also forgot his work."
Equally important
समान रूप से महत्वपूर्ण
Signals that the next point carries the same weight or significance as the previous one.
Quality control is crucial, but equally important is ensuring timely delivery to customers.
What's more
इससे ज्यादा और क्या
Introduces an additional point that supports or expands on the current one, often with emphasis.
The phone has a powerful processor. What's more, it features a high-resolution camera.
Apart from that
इसके अलावा
Introduces a point that is separate from what was just mentioned, adding to the discussion.
The product got positive reviews. Apart from that, it has gained a strong social media following.
Either… or
या तो यह या वह
Presents a choice between exactly two possibilities. Positive construction.
You can either go to the gym in the morning or exercise at home in the evening.
Pair: Use "neither…nor" for negative construction.
Neither… nor
न तो… न ही
Used when denying two possibilities simultaneously. Negative counterpart to "either…or".
The candidate possesses neither the required experience nor the necessary qualifications.
Grammar: Verb agrees with the subject nearest to "nor" — "Neither he nor they ARE coming."
Along with / Together with
साथ में / के साथ साथ
"Along with" adds something to a group; "Together with" indicates people or things acting as a unit.
She excels in maths and science, along with her passion for literature.
Note: Like "as well as", these do not change subject-verb agreement of the main subject.
To be specific
विस्तार से / विशेष रूप से
Used to emphasize that you are about to provide precise, exact details or examples.
The company faces multiple issues. To be specific, its cash flow has declined by 30% this quarter.

Contrasting
19 connectors — विरोधाभास / अंतर दर्शाना

Use these to show a difference, contradiction, or unexpected outcome between two ideas. The most tested category in competitive exams!

However
तथापि / फिर भी
Introduces a contrasting or opposing idea. The most common and versatile contrast connector in formal English.
He is often busy; however, he always makes time for his family.
Punctuation: After a semicolon: "He tried; however, he failed." OR as a sentence starter: "However, he failed."
Nevertheless / Nonetheless
फिर भी / बहरहाल
Acknowledges a counter-argument but shows the main statement still holds true. "Nonetheless" is slightly more formal.
The weather was unpredictable; nonetheless, they went ahead with their outdoor picnic.
Difference: Both are interchangeable. "Nevertheless" is marginally more common in academic writing.
In contrast / On the contrary
इसके विपरीत
"In contrast" highlights differences between two things. "On the contrary" directly contradicts the previous statement.
In contrast to his earlier statement, he now claims no knowledge of the situation.
Key difference: "On the contrary" corrects a misunderstanding; "In contrast" merely compares two different things.
But
लेकिन / परंतु
The simplest contrast connector. Introduces a contrasting idea within the same sentence.
He studied for hours, but he still didn't pass the exam.
Tip: "But" is informal. In formal writing, prefer "however" or "nevertheless".
While / Whereas
जबकि / इसी दौरान
"While" can show contrast OR concurrent actions. "Whereas" specifically shows contrast between two different things.
While she was outgoing and social, her sister was more reserved and quiet.
Distinction: "Whereas he is tall, she is short" = pure contrast. "While he was sleeping, she was working" = simultaneous.
Conversely
इसके विपरीत / उल्टा
Introduces an idea that is the opposite or reverse of the previous one. More formal than "on the other hand".
The model promised fuel efficiency; conversely, it was criticised for its lack of safety features.
Yet
अभी तक / फिर भी
Introduces something surprising that contrasts with what was just said. Similar to "but" but slightly more formal.
The restaurant had a limited menu, yet the dishes were exceptionally delicious.
Note: "Yet" can also mean "up until now" in a different context — "I haven't finished yet."
Albeit
यद्यपि / हालांकि
Formal word meaning "although" or "even though". Used to introduce a concession.
The company showed consistent growth, albeit at a slower rate than expected.
Register: Formal/literary. Rarely used in spoken English.
Although / Though / Even though
हालांकि / यद्यपि / फिर भी
All introduce a subordinate clause that contrasts with the main clause. "Even though" is the most emphatic of the three.
Although they disagreed on many issues, they maintained a respectful friendship.
Scale: though (weakest) → although (medium) → even though (most emphatic)
In spite of / Despite
बावजूद / इसके बावजूद
Shows that something happens even though there is a factor that might prevent it. Used with nouns or gerunds, not clauses.
Despite the economic downturn, the company managed to increase its profits.
Grammar: "Despite/In spite of + noun/gerund" — NOT "despite that". For clauses, use "although/even though".
Alternatively
वैकल्पिक / अन्यथा
Suggests another possibility or choice as an alternative to what was previously mentioned.
You can take the train to the city, or alternatively, you can drive there.
Notwithstanding
बावजूद / तथापि
Very formal term meaning "in spite of" or "regardless of". Common in legal and official writing.
Notwithstanding the challenges, the project was completed successfully.
Register: Highly formal. Found in legal contracts: "Notwithstanding any other provision of this agreement…"
Still
फिर भी / अभी भी
Used to indicate something continues or remains true despite obstacles or expectations.
Despite the stormy weather, they decided to go camping and still had a great time.
In any case
किसी भी स्थिति में / बहरहाल
Indicates something is true or will happen regardless of other circumstances. Used to dismiss a concern or add a definitive point.
The weather forecast predicts rain. In any case, we should bring umbrellas.

Cause & Effect
7 connectors — कारण और परिणाम

These connect a cause (reason) to its result. Essential for essay writing, reports, and analysis questions.

Therefore
इसलिए / अतः
Indicates a logical cause-and-effect relationship. The most direct way to show a conclusion follows from evidence.
The weather was extremely hot; therefore, they decided to stay indoors.
Synonyms: Hence, thus, consequently, as a result — all express cause-effect but with different formality levels.
Consequently
फलस्वरूप / परिणामस्वरूप
Signals a result or consequence of a specific previous cause. More formal and precise than "so".
The heavy rain caused flooding; consequently, many homes were damaged.
As a result
इस परिणामस्वरूप / नतीजतन
Highlights the consequence or outcome that follows from a previous action or situation.
As a result of the new policies, carbon emissions have decreased significantly.
Usage: "As a result of [noun]" OR used alone to start a consequence sentence.
Because / As / Since
क्योंकि / जैसे / चूंकि
All introduce the reason/cause behind a result. "Because" is strongest; "since/as" imply the reason is already known.
Since the concert tickets were selling out quickly, we purchased them online to secure our seats.
Key: "Because" answers "Why?" directly. "Since/As" are used when the reason is less central to the sentence.
Due to
इस कारण / के कारण
Indicates the reason or cause behind a particular result. Used with nouns, not clauses.
The flight was cancelled due to bad weather conditions.
Grammar: "Due to + noun" — "Due to rain, the match was cancelled." NOT "Due to it rained."
Hence / Thus
इसलिए / अतः
"Hence" and "thus" are formal/academic equivalents of "therefore". "Thus" is especially common in scientific and mathematical writing.
The data was incomplete; hence, the conclusions drawn cannot be considered reliable.
Formality: thus > hence > therefore > so. Use "thus" in academic papers.
In consequence
परिणामस्वरूप
Formal phrase pointing to a result that follows from a previous action or situation. Less common than "as a result".
The company ignored safety regulations; in consequence, it faced heavy penalties from the authorities.

Similarity
3 connectors — समानता दर्शाना

Show that two ideas, situations, or cases share common features or follow the same pattern.

Similarly
उसी प्रकार / इसी तरह
Indicates a similarity or parallel between two ideas, situations, or points.
Similarly, he excels in both maths and science, showing balanced academic strengths.
Use in essays: "X does Y. Similarly, Z does W." — great for comparing two entities.
In a similar fashion / Likewise
इसी तरह / उसी प्रकार
Suggests that a similar approach or pattern is being followed. "Likewise" is shorter and more common in speech.
Just as the first project was managed efficiently, we aim to handle this one in a similar fashion.
In the same vein
एक ही शिरे में / उसी संदर्भ में
Used to introduce a point that is similar in style, tone, or content to the previous one.
The marketing team is focused on online presence; in the same vein, we are exploring social media ads.
Note: More formal/literary than "similarly". Often used in academic or business writing.

Condition Based
6 connectors — शर्त / यदि-तो

These introduce a condition that must be met for something to happen. Crucial for "if-then" logic and persuasive writing.

If
अगर / यदि
The simplest conditional connector. Introduces a condition that must be met for a result to occur.
If it rains, we will stay indoors.
Types: Zero conditional (facts), First (real future), Second (unreal present), Third (unreal past).
Even if
भले ही / चाहे
Introduces a condition that, even if met, may not prevent or guarantee a result. Stronger than "if".
She would attend the concert even if it meant travelling a long distance.
Difference: "If" = there's a real possibility. "Even if" = I don't care about the condition, the result stays the same.
Unless
जब तक न / यदि न
Introduces a negative condition — something that must be satisfied to avoid a negative consequence. Means "if not".
We will go for a picnic unless it rains. (= if it does NOT rain)
Key: Never use "unless…not" together. "Unless it doesn't rain" is wrong. "Unless it rains" is correct.
Provided (that)
बशर्ते / यदि… तो
Introduces a condition that must be fulfilled to allow a particular action or result. More formal than "if".
You can borrow my car provided that you return it by tomorrow.
Synonyms: "Providing (that)", "on condition that", "as long as" — all express a firm condition.
In case
यदि / अगर (सावधानी के लिए)
Used to introduce a precautionary condition — you do something to prepare for a possible situation.
Bring an umbrella in case it rains.
Difference from "if": "Bring an umbrella if it rains" (reactive). "Bring an umbrella in case it rains" (proactive precaution).
As long as
जब तक कि / जब तक
Sets a condition that must be continuously maintained for a result to continue. Ongoing condition.
You can use my computer as long as you don't delete any files.

Showing Sequence / Time
2 connectors — क्रम / समय दिखाना

Show simultaneous actions or that one event occurs in the middle of another.

In the meantime
इस बीच में / इसी दौरान
Indicates that something is happening concurrently while another event or action takes place. Suggests a time gap.
The report is being reviewed. In the meantime, let's prepare the presentation slides.
Also: "Meanwhile" is the shorter synonym used more in storytelling and news writing.
At the same time
एक ही समय पर / साथ ही
Highlights that two or more actions or events are happening simultaneously. Can also introduce a contrasting idea.
She was managing the project and at the same time training the new team members.
Dual use: Also used for contrast: "He's brilliant; at the same time, he can be difficult to work with."

Concluding / Summarizing
7 connectors — निष्कर्ष / सारांश

Use these in the final paragraph of essays, reports, or speeches to wrap up your arguments and draw conclusions.

To sum up
सारांश में / संक्षेप में
Summarizes the key points made in the preceding discussion. Slightly more informal than "in conclusion".
To sum up, the three key factors driving the company's success are innovation, quality, and customer focus.
In conclusion
निष्कर्ष के तौर पर
Signals the final paragraph where you wrap up the discussion and restate the main points or thesis.
In conclusion, renewable energy is not just an option but a necessity for sustainable development.
Essay tip: Never introduce new arguments after "in conclusion" — it's only for wrapping up.
On the whole
कुल मिलाकर
Provides a general summary or overall assessment of a discussion, taking most factors into account.
On the whole, the policy has been effective, though there is room for improvement in rural areas.
All things considered
सब बातों पर विचार करते हुए
Indicates you are taking various factors into account when making a statement or judgement.
All things considered, investing in public transport infrastructure remains the most viable long-term solution.
To put it differently
इसे भिन्न प्रकार से कहें तो
Introduces a rephrased or alternative way of expressing the same point. Useful for clarifying complex ideas.
The process is iterative. To put it differently, you improve the product through repeated cycles of testing and feedback.
In this regard / In view of
इस संबंध में / इस दृष्टि से
"In this regard" refers to the specific aspect being discussed. "In view of" signals that context or perspective should be considered.
In view of the rising inflation, the government has decided to review its fiscal policies.
Overall / In brief
कुल मिलाकर / संक्षेप में
"Overall" gives a general final assessment. "In brief" signals a concise summary of key points.
Overall, the experiment was a success, confirming our initial hypothesis about temperature effects.

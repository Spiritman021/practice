Section 1

What are Connectors?

A connector is any word or phrase that joins two sentences, clauses, or ideas together. Connectors create logical relationships between ideas — contrast, addition, cause, condition, etc.

Connector: Any word or phrase (conjunction, preposition, or adverb) that joins two ideas or sentences. It can appear at the beginning, middle, or end of a sentence.

Starter: A special type of connector that can only appear at the beginning of a sentence. All starters are connectors, but not all connectors are starters.
Key Relationship All starters are connectors — but not all connectors are starters. A starter is a subset of connectors that can only link sentences from the start. This distinction is crucial for Para Jumbles.
Connectors ≠ Conjunctions Every connector is not a conjunction. Prepositions also act as connectors (e.g., despite, but used as a preposition). Connectors are a broader group that includes conjunctions, prepositions, and adverbs.
Section 2

Four Categories of Connectors

Every connector belongs to one of four major categories. Knowing the category tells you exactly how the connector is being used and what type of relationship it creates.

⚡ Contrast Links two opposite or conflicting ideas
➕ Addition Adds more information to an existing idea
🔗 Result & Reason Shows cause and effect relationships
🔀 Conditional Places a condition or limitation on an action
Section 3

Category 1 — Contrast Connectors

Contrast connectors join two sentences that contain opposing or contradictory ideas. Even though the two ideas conflict, we accept both as true. The common Hindi meaning is: के बावजूद / फिर भी / हालांकि

All Contrast Connectors Contrast
However While / Whilst In spite of Despite Even so Even then Whereas Nevertheless Nonetheless Notwithstanding Although / Though Even though Yet Still But All the same After all

The Important Trio: Notwithstanding, Nevertheless & Nonetheless

These three are the most commonly confused contrast connectors. They all mean "despite that / even so / in spite of" but their structure differs.

Notwithstanding
के बावजूद / In spite of
Used before a noun/noun phrase. Can appear at the beginning or middle of a sentence.

Notwithstanding the extreme cold, he left the house wearing shorts.

इतनी ठंड के बावजूद वह शॉर्ट्स पहन कर निकल गया।

Notwithstanding the fact that he is inexperienced, I will hire him.

इस बात के बावजूद कि उसे अनुभव नहीं है, मैं उसे रखूंगा।
Nevertheless
फिर भी / Even so
Used between two complete sentences/clauses. Typically in the middle.

She was tired; nevertheless, she decided to go to work.

वह थकी हुई थी, फिर भी उसने काम पर जाने का फैसला किया।
Nonetheless
फिर भी / Still
Interchangeable with nevertheless. Slightly more formal.

She was scared; nonetheless, she tried to open the door.

वह डरी हुई थी, फिर भी उसने दरवाजा खोलने की कोशिश की।

More Contrast Connectors Explained

Connector Hindi Meaning Example
However हालांकि / फिर भी He is not well. However, he will attend the session.
Although / Though / Even though हालांकि / यद्यपि Although he is not well, he will attend the session.
While / Whilst / Whereas जबकि (contrast) / जब (time) I am a doctor while my sister is an inspector. (contrast)
In spite of / Despite के बावजूद Despite the rain, he went out. (followed by noun/gerund)
Yet / Still फिर भी / अभी भी He lost the game; yet he enjoyed it.
But लेकिन (conjunction) / सिवाय (preposition) He is not well but will attend the session.
All but Ram went to the party. (except Ram)
All the same / After all फिर भी / आखिरकार He failed; all the same, he kept trying.
⚠ Special Note: While
  • While belongs to BOTH contrast AND addition categories.
  • When it means "हालांकि" (two opposing things) → Contrast category
  • When it means "जब" (two things happening simultaneously) → Addition category
  • Example (Addition): "I am teaching while she is cooking." (same time)
  • Example (Contrast): "I am a doctor while my sister is a police officer." (opposite roles)
Section 4

Category 2 — Addition Connectors

Addition connectors are used when one sentence adds more information to another. Both sentences support or extend the same idea.

All Addition Connectors Addition
And Besides Not only… but also Neither… nor As well as Moreover Furthermore In addition to Along with Together with Also / Even / Too Apart from While (same time) So (meaning "and")
Connector Hindi Meaning Example
Besides के अलावा / इसके अलावा He is a doctor. Besides, he is also an actor.
Not only… but also ना केवल… बल्कि… भी Not only teachers but also the principal enjoyed the party.
Neither… nor ना यह… ना वह Neither she nor he is coming. (can also appear separately)
As well as के साथ-साथ / भी She is a singer as well as a dancer.
Moreover / Furthermore इसके अलावा / और अधिक The plan is cost-effective. Moreover, it is quick.
Along with / Together with के साथ He came along with his brother.
Apart from / In addition to के अलावा / अतिरिक्त Apart from cooking, she also paints.
Further (also addition) इसके अतिरिक्त The government should do this. Further, it should also do that.
Special Case: Neither / Nor "Neither" and "nor" can appear separately (not always as a pair). Example: "I don't like tea." → "I don't like it nor does she like it." They can also appear together: "I like neither tea nor coffee."
Important: So has two meanings "So" can belong to Addition (meaning "and therefore / also") AND to Result (meaning "इसलिए / thus"). Pay attention to context.
Addition: "I like tea and so does she." | Result: "It was raining, so I stayed home."
Section 5

Category 3 — Result & Reason Connectors

These connectors show a cause-and-effect relationship. Some connectors always introduce the result/effect, while others always introduce the reason/cause.

The Golden Rule

  • Result Connectors → always followed by the RESULT (effect/outcome)
  • Reason Connectors → always followed by the REASON (cause)
  • You CANNOT use a result connector before a reason and vice versa.

Result Connectors — always followed by the effect

So Therefore Thus Hence As a result Consequently As a consequence Subsequently Sequentially Thereby

I was not well; therefore, I did not meet him.

मैं ठीक नहीं था — इसलिए मैं उससे नहीं मिला। (reason → result)

He burnt the midnight oil; hence, he cleared the RBI Grade B exam.

उसने बहुत मेहनत की — इसलिए उसने परीक्षा पास की।

Reason Connectors — always followed by the cause

Because Since As Due to Due to the fact that Owing to For As a result of

I did not meet him because I was not well.

मैं उससे नहीं मिला — क्योंकि मैं ठीक नहीं था। (result → reason)

Since it was not well, I did not go outside.

चूंकि मैं ठीक नहीं था, इसलिए मैं बाहर नहीं गया।
Type Connector Hindi Meaning What follows it?
Result So / Therefore / Thus / Hence इसलिए / अतः Always the RESULT
Result As a result / Consequently परिणामस्वरूप Always the RESULT
Result Subsequently / Sequentially बाद में / क्रमानुसार Always the RESULT
Reason Because / Since / As क्योंकि / चूंकि Always the REASON
Reason Due to / Owing to की वजह से Always the REASON
Reason For / As a result of क्योंकि / के फलस्वरूप Always the REASON
⚠ Para Jumble Tip
  • In Para Jumbles, the sentence with therefore / hence / so / thus can never be the first sentence — it always needs a cause before it.
  • The sentence with because / since / as is a reason — look for its result elsewhere in the paragraph.

As far as … is concerned

As far as I am concerned, I will not vote for him.

जहां तक मेरी बात है, मैं उसके लिए वोट नहीं करूंगी।
Section 6

Category 4 — Conditional Connectors

Conditional connectors place a condition or stipulation on an action. They describe scenarios where one thing happens only if another condition is met.

All Conditional Connectors Conditional
If Unless Until / Till By the time Otherwise Else Whether… or Provided / Providing So that / Such that Supposing Lest Scarcely… when Hardly… when / before No sooner… than
Connector Hindi Meaning Example
Unless जब तक नहीं / यदि नहीं Unless you study hard, you will fail.
Until / Till जब तक I will wait until you arrive.
Otherwise / Else अन्यथा / नहीं तो Study hard; otherwise, you will fail.
Otherwise/Else = alternative outcome
Provided / Providing (that) बशर्ते कि / इस शर्त पर I will come to your house, provided that you give me your notes.
Lest कहीं ऐसा न हो / कि ना हो Look at the map, lest you get lost.
Note: After "lest" → only "should" or bare verb, never "will/can/might"
Whether… or चाहे… या / या तो… या Please tell me whether you are coming or not.
So that जिससे कि / ताकि We work hard so that we can live well.
Such… that इतना… कि It is such cold weather that I cannot go outside.
Hardly/Scarcely… when/before मुश्किल से ही… तभी Hardly had I entered the room when it started raining.
No sooner… than जैसे ही… वैसे ही No sooner did he arrive than the show began.
By the time जब तक / उस वक्त तक By the time the sun rises, I will finish this.
⚠ Special Rule for "Lest"
  • After lest, always use should or a bare infinitive. Never use will, can, might, would.
  • ✅ Correct: "Look at the map lest you should get lost." / "…lest you get lost."
  • ❌ Wrong: "…lest you will get lost." / "…lest you might get lost."
So that vs. Such that:
— "So that" → used to express purpose. ("We work so that we can earn.")
— "So… that" (split) → used to express degree/consequence. ("It is so cold that I cannot go.")
— "Such… that" → same as above but with a noun. ("It is such cold weather that…")
Section 7

Special Rules & Important Notes

Connectors that CANNOT start a sentence (must appear in the middle)

Some connectors are not starters. They can NEVER appear at the very beginning of a sentence/paragraph. In Para Jumbles, if a sentence begins with these words, it cannot be the first sentence.

Cannot Be Used at the Start of a Paragraph

  • However — must follow a contrasting sentence
  • Moreover / Furthermore / Further — must follow a sentence with related info
  • Therefore / Hence / Thus / So — must follow the reason sentence
  • Consequently / Subsequently — must follow the cause
  • Nevertheless / Nonetheless — must follow the opposing idea
  • But / Yet (when used mid-sentence) — links clauses, needs a preceding one
Para Jumble Strategy
  • If a sentence starts with However, Moreover, Therefore, Hence, Thus, Nevertheless, Consequently → it is definitely NOT the first sentence.
  • The sentence with a result connector (so/therefore/thus/hence) often comes after the sentence with a reason connector (because/since/as).
  • Suggestion sentences (government should do this) often come towards the end of a paragraph.
  • Introduction/topic sentences introducing a problem come first.

While — Dual Category Connector

While can mean two very different things depending on context:

While [Contrast] → I am a doctor while my sister is a police officer.

मैं डॉक्टर हूं जबकि मेरी बहन पुलिस अधिकारी है। (विरोध)

While [Addition/Time] → I am teaching while she is cooking.

मैं पढ़ा रही हूं जब कि वह खाना बना रही है। (एक साथ)

Scarcely/Hardly/No Sooner — Inversion Rule

When Hardly / Scarcely / No sooner appear at the start of a sentence, the sentence uses inversion (auxiliary before subject):

Hardly had I entered the room when it started raining.
No sooner had he sat down than the phone rang.
Scarcely had she finished speaking when the audience applauded.

Also / Even / Too — Position Matters

These addition connectors appear at specific positions:

I like tea. Also, I like coffee.

I like tea and she likes it too.

Even the smallest mistake matters.

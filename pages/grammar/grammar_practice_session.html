1
Superfluous Modifiers (May + Possibly)

When using modal auxiliaries that express uncertainty (such as may or might), adding adverbs of similar probability like possibly or likely in the same clause is redundant.

❌
They may possibly postpone their vacation after the news... Incorrect: "may" and "possibly" are redundant together.
✅
They may postpone their vacation after the news... Correct: Remove "possibly" to maintain conciseness.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "May" का मतलब पहले से ही 'शायद' या संभावना होता है। इसलिए वाक्य में दोबारा "possibly" लगाना अनावश्यक (superfluous) है। दोनों में से किसी एक को ही प्रयोग में लाएं।
2
Practice (Noun) vs Practise (Verb)

In British/Indian English, words ending in -ce are nouns, while those ending in -se are verbs. Examples include practice/practise, advice/advise, and device/devise. Infinitives require the verb spelling (to + V1).

❌
The teacher asked the (A) / students to practice the (B) / questions provided by (C) / her during the class test. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: The noun "practice" in part (B) is used after the infinitive marker "to" (to + practice).
✅
The teacher asked the (A) / students to practise the (B) / questions provided by (C) / her during the class test. (D) / No error (E) Correct: "practise" (with 's') is the correct verb spelling for "to practise".
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): अभ्यास करने की बात हो रही है, यानी क्रिया (Action)। 'to' के साथ V1 आता है, इसलिए C-form (Noun) की जगह S-form 'practise' (Verb) का उपयोग करें।
3
Subjunctive Verb after "It is High Time"

When the phrase It is high time, It is time, or It is about time is followed by a subject, the verb must be in the simple past tense (V2) to convey that the action is long overdue.

❌
It is high time children (A) / should realise that getting (B) / addicted to social media is not (C) / going to help them with their future. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: The auxiliary phrase "should realise" is used in part (B) after "It is high time + subject", but we must use V2 directly.
✅
It is high time children (A) / realised that getting (B) / addicted to social media is not (C) / going to help them with their future. (D) / No error (E) Correct: "realised" (V2) is used directly, omitting "should".
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): जब भी वाक्य में 'It is high time' के तुरंत बाद कोई Subject आ जाए, तो क्रिया की 2nd form (V2) लगाई जाती है। यदि Subject न होकर सीधा preposition 'to' हो, तो V1 लगाते हैं।
4
Fixed Preposition: Engulfed IN

The verb engulf takes the fixed preposition in rather than the agent preposition by when representing containment by flames, water, or clouds.

❌
His entire house was (A) / engulfed by the fire that (B) / accidentally broke out in (C) / the society last night. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: "engulfed by" in part (B) is grammatically incorrect in competitive exams; the verb "engulf" takes the fixed preposition "in".
✅
His entire house was (A) / engulfed in the fire that (B) / accidentally broke out in (C) / the society last night. (D) / No error (E) Correct: "engulfed in" is the correct fixed preposition construction.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): आग कोई सजीव Agent नहीं है जिसने काम किया हो, बल्कि घर आग में घिर चुका था। इसलिए 'engulfed' के साथ हमेशा fixed preposition 'in' का ही प्रयोग करें।
5
Question Tag for "I am"

A positive sentence starting with I am is paired with the negative question tag aren't I?. This is because a contracted form like amn't does not exist in standard English.

❌
I am good at performing (A) / such challenging (B) / and risk oriented (C) / tasks, isn't I? (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: The question tag "isn't I?" in part (D) is grammatically incorrect. Positive statements starting with "I am" require the tag "aren't I?".
✅
I am good at performing (A) / such challenging (B) / and risk oriented (C) / tasks, aren't I? (D) / No error (E) Correct: "aren't I?" is the standard correct negative tag for "I am".
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): 'I am' का negative tag 'amn't I' होना चाहिए था, पर चूँकि 'amn't' कोई शब्द नहीं है, इसलिए हम 'aren't I' का उपयोग करते हैं। ध्यान रखें, अगर वाक्य नकारात्मक हो (I am not), तो tag साधारण 'am I' ही रहता है।
6
Fraction Subject-Verb Agreement

When fractions or percentages act as subjects, the verb agrees with the noun that follows the preposition of. If that noun is plural, the verb must be plural.

❌
One-fourth of the employees has raised a query... Incorrect: "has" is singular, but "employees" is plural.
✅
One-fourth of the employees have raised a query... Correct: Use plural verb "have" to match the plural subject noun.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): जब भी fraction या percentage के साथ 'of' लिखा हो, तो 'of' के बाद वाले Noun के हिसाब से Verb आएगी। 'employees' plural है, इसलिए 'have' आएगा।
7
Dangling Participles (It being...)

A participle clause (e.g. starting with Being...) must have a logical subject. If the subject of the participle clause differs from the main clause subject, we must prefix the participle with a pronoun like It.

❌
Being a dicey situation, the board of directors could not... Incorrect: Makes it sound like "the board of directors" is a dicey situation.
✅
It being a dicey situation, the board of directors could not... Correct: Prepend "It" to act as the subject of the participle clause.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): अगर हम 'It' नहीं लगाएंगे, तो participle clause का subject भी मुख्य वाक्य का subject 'board of directors' मान लिया जाएगा, जो कि गलत है। इसलिए सिचुएशन के लिए 'It' का उपयोग करें।
8
Divided Collective Noun Verb Agreement

Collective nouns (like committee, board, jury) take a singular verb when they act as a unified entity, but require a plural verb when their members are divided in opinion or action.

❌
The committee of finance is divided on the investment. Incorrect: "divided" indicates non-unity, requiring a plural verb.
✅
The committee of finance are/were divided on the investment. Correct: Plural verb 'are' or 'were' is correct for division.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): यदि कमेटी एक साथ मिलकर कोई निर्णय ले रही है तो singular verb, पर यदि वे बटे हुए हैं (divided) तो plural verb (are / were) का उपयोग होता है।
9
Pronoun Case in Comparative Clauses

In comparative clauses using than or as, the cases of the pronouns being compared must be equivalent. Compare a subject pronoun with a subject pronoun.

❌
They think that I am more intelligent than her. Incorrect: Compares the subjective pronoun "I" with the objective pronoun "her".
✅
They think that I am more intelligent than she. Correct: Compares subjective pronoun "I" with subjective "she" (implied: "than she is").
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): तुलना हमेशा बराबर की चीजों में होती है। चूँकि तुलना 'I' (subjective case) से हो रही है, इसलिए 'than' के बाद भी subjective case (she) का प्रयोग करें, न कि 'her' (objective case) का।
10
Prepositions before Adverbs of Place (Abroad)

The word abroad functions as an adverb of place when modifying verbs like go, travel, or live. Since it is an adverb and not a noun, it does not take a direction preposition like to or the article the before it.

❌
Vrunali is going to abroad for higher studies... Incorrect: The preposition "to" is placed before the adverb "abroad".
✅
Neha is upset because next week Vrunali is going abroad for higher studies. Correct: "is going abroad" is correct without prepositions. The sentence is grammatically correct (No error).
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "Abroad" एक Adverb of Place है, Noun नहीं। इसलिए इसके पहले "to" या "the" नहीं लगाया जाता (e.g. go to abroad गलत है, go abroad सही है)। दिया गया वाक्य बिल्कुल सही है, इसलिए इसमें कोई त्रुटि नहीं है (No error)।
11
Relative Pronouns with "The Same" (That)

When a noun is preceded by expressions like the same, the only, or all, the relative pronoun that must be used to refer to it, instead of who or which.

❌
Rohit is the same boy (A) / who led the protest against (B) / racial discrimination within (C) / the college campus. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: "who" is used in part (B) after the phrase "the same boy".
✅
Rohit is the same boy (A) / that led the protest against (B) / racial discrimination within (C) / the college campus. (D) / No error (E) Correct: "that" is the correct relative pronoun after "the same".
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): जब भी वाक्य में "the same" या "the only" शब्द आ जाए, तो उसके बाद सजीव या निर्जीव दोनों के लिए relative pronoun "that" का ही प्रयोग किया जाता है, "who" या "which" का नहीं।
12
Redundancy (Final Conclusion)

The word conclusion itself refers to a final decision, resolution, or outcome. Preceding it with the adjective final is superfluous (redundant).

❌
After an hour of being (A) / in a state of dilemma, (B) / the team of doctors (C) / reached the final conclusion. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: "final conclusion" in part (D) is a redundant phrase.
✅
After an hour of being (A) / in a state of dilemma, (B) / the team of doctors (C) / reached the conclusion. (D) / No error (E) Correct: Delete "final" to maintain conciseness.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "Conclusion" (निष्कर्ष) हमेशा आखिरी (final) ही होता है। इसलिए इसके साथ "final" शब्द का प्रयोग करना Superfluous (redundant) है। केवल "reached the conclusion" या "reached a conclusion" का प्रयोग करें।
13
The Little vs A Little

While a little means a small quantity (some), the little refers to a specific small quantity—meaning "all of the small amount that was there." When referring to a specific quantity that was saved and then affected, use the little.

❌
A little juice I (A) / saved for him was (B) / spilled on the floor (C) / by the naughty kids. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: "A little" in part (A) implies just some general amount, but we mean the specific quantity that I saved.
✅
The little juice I (A) / saved for him was (B) / spilled on the floor (C) / by the naughty kids. (D) / No error (E) Correct: "The little" specifies the entire small amount of juice that was saved.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "A little" का मतलब होता है थोड़ा सा (positive sense में)। "The little" का मतलब होता है 'जो भी थोड़ा सा बचा या रखा था वो सब'। चूँकि वह सारा जूस जो मैंने उसके लिए बचाया था, गिर गया, इसलिए यहाँ "the little" का प्रयोग होगा।
14
Correlative Conjunctions (No Sooner... Than)

The conjunction phrase No sooner must always be paired with than to introduce the subsequent action. Avoid pairing it with when or then.

❌
No sooner had he stepped (A) / down the stage when the (B) / crowd started approaching (C) / him for the autograph. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: "when" is used in part (B) to pair with "No sooner".
✅
No sooner had he stepped (A) / down the stage than the (B) / crowd started approaching (C) / him for the autograph. (D) / No error (E) Correct: Pair "No sooner" with "than".
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "No sooner" के साथ हमेशा "than" (T-H-A-N) का प्रयोग होता है, "when" या "then" का नहीं। "Hardly" और "Scarcely" के साथ "when" का प्रयोग किया जाता है।
15
Articles with Nationalities (The French)

When using an adjective of nationality (like French, German, English) to represent the collective people or army of that country, the definite article the must precede the word.

❌
The Germans fought and (A) / defeated French in the Franco-Prussian (B) / war... Incorrect: "French" in part (B) lacks the definite article "the".
✅
The Germans fought and (A) / defeated the French in the Franco-Prussian (B) / war... Correct: "the French" refers to the French nation/people collectively (parallel to "The Germans").
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): जब हम किसी देश के लोगों या वहाँ की सेना की बात करते हैं, तो उस राष्ट्रीयता दर्शाने वाले शब्द के पहले "the" लगाते हैं (जैसे: The Germans, The French, The Indians)। इसलिए "defeated French" की जगह "defeated the French" होगा।
16
Between... And vs From... To

The word between must always be paired with the conjunction and to connect boundaries. In contrast, the word from is paired with to. Avoid cross-coupling them (like between X to Y).

❌
The latest reports suggested that (A) / one should not step out in the Sun (B) / between 2 p.m to 4 p.m due (C) / to the presence of strong heat waves. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: "between" is paired with "to" in part (C).
✅
The latest reports suggested that (A) / one should not step out in the Sun (B) / between 2 p.m and 4 p.m due (C) / to the presence of strong heat waves. (D) / No error (E) Correct: Pair "between" with "and" (or use "from 2 p.m. to 4 p.m.").
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "Between" के साथ हमेशा "and" का जोड़ा (pair) बनता है, "to" का नहीं (e.g. between 2 p.m and 4 p.m)। अगर "from" का प्रयोग किया जाता है, तब उसके साथ "to" लगाते हैं (e.g. from 2 p.m to 4 p.m)।
17
Definite Article with General Plural Nouns

Do not use the definite article the before plural abstract nouns (like values) or general classes of things when they are referred to in a general sense.

❌
She was born of Indian (A) / parents, yet she lived most (B) / of her life abroad and (C) / cherished the foreign values. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: "the" is used before "foreign values" in a general statement in part (D).
✅
She was born of Indian (A) / parents, yet she lived most (B) / of her life abroad and (C) / cherished foreign values. (D) / No error (E) Correct: Omit "the" since no specific values are pre-defined.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "Values" (मूल्य) यहाँ एक abstract plural noun है जिसका उपयोग सामान्य (general) अर्थ में किया गया है, किसी विशिष्ट (specific) मूल्यों के लिए नहीं। इसलिए इसके पहले definite article "the" का प्रयोग गलत है।
18
Adverb of Manner for Verbs

Words ending in -ly like cowardly, friendly, and miserly are adjectives, not adverbs. To modify a verb (like behave), use the adverbial phrase in a cowardly manner/way.

❌
Everybody was disappointed (A) / as they did not expect (B) / him to behave cowardly (C) / on the battlefield. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: Adjective "cowardly" in part (C) is used to modify the verb "behave".
✅
Everybody was disappointed (A) / as they did not expect (B) / him to behave in a cowardly manner (C) / on the battlefield. (D) / No error (E) Correct: Use the adverbial phrase "in a cowardly manner" to modify the verb.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "Cowardly" (कायर) एक Adjective है, Adverb नहीं। Verb (behave) की विशेषता बताने के लिए हमें Adverb की आवश्यकता होती है। इसलिए "behave cowardly" गलत है, सही रूप "behave in a cowardly manner" (कायरतापूर्ण तरीके से) होगा।
19
Word Order in Indirect Questions

In indirect or reported speech questions (sentences that do not end in a question mark), we use assertive word order (Subject + Verb) rather than interrogative inversion (Verb + Subject).

❌
I asked her what had she (A) done to her face (B) / in the name of (C) / plastic surgery. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: "what had she" uses inverted question order in an indirect question in part (A).
✅
I asked her what she had (A) done to her face (B) / in the name of (C) / plastic surgery. (D) / No error (E) Correct: "what she had" maintains standard assertive word order.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): जब कोई प्रश्न Indirect Speech (अप्रत्यक्ष कथन) में होता है, तो वाक्य Assertive (साधारण) बन जाता है, यानी पहले Subject (she) और फिर Verb (had) आएगी। Direct Question की तरह inversion (had she) नहीं किया जाता।
20
Loath (Adjective) vs Loathe (Verb)

Loath (adjective) means reluctant or unwilling. Loathe (verb) means to hate or dislike intensely. After a linking verb (like was), the adjective form must be used.

❌
The little girl was absolutely loathe (A) / to drink vegetable (B) / juice and eat homemade (C) / food for two months. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: Verb "loathe" in part (A) is used after the linking verb "was".
✅
The little girl was absolutely loat (A) / to drink vegetable (B) / juice and eat homemade (C) / food for two months. (D) / No error (E) Correct: Adjective "loath" correctly expresses reluctance.
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "Loath" (बिना 'e' के) एक Adjective है जिसका अर्थ 'अनिच्छुक' होता है। "Loathe" ('e' के साथ) एक Verb है जिसका अर्थ 'घृणा करना' होता है। यहाँ linking verb (was) के बाद Adjective "loath" का प्रयोग होगा।
21
Lest... Should

The conjunction lest (meaning "for fear that" or "otherwise") is always paired with the modal verb should or a subjunctive base verb, never with would, will, or may.

❌
The coach told (A) / Peter to run fast (B) / lest he would (C) / lose the race. (D) / No error (E) Incorrect: "would" is paired with "lest" in part (C).
✅
The coach told (A) / Peter to run fast (B) / lest he should (C) / lose the race. (D) / No error (E) Correct: "lest" is correctly paired with "should".
Nimisha's explanation (Hindi): "Lest" के साथ हमेशा "should" का ही प्रयोग किया जाता है, "would", "will" या "may" का नहीं। चूँकि "lest" नकारात्मक भाव देता है, इसलिए इसके क्लॉज़ में "not" का प्रयोग भी नहीं होता।

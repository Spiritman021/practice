The 10 Core Vocabulary Slides

Invidious (Adjective)

Hindi: भेदभावपूर्ण
English: Promoting or exhibiting an unfair, highly biased stance calculated to cause deep public resentment, anger, or systemic jealousy.
Synonyms: Discriminatory, Resentful, Prejudicial, Biased, Partial
Antonyms: Equitable, Impartial, Fair, Just

Extol (Verb)

Hindi: भर-भर के तारीफ करना
English: To publicly celebrate, applaud, or commend someone's structural feats enthusiastically.
Synonyms: Eulogize, Laud, Glorify, Exalt, Appreciate, Applaud, Commend
Antonyms: Denounce, Vilify, Condemn, Criticize

Affectation (Noun)

Hindi: दिखावा या बनावटीपन
English: An artificial, highly theatrical social behavior or unnatural posture adopted deliberately to impress others; wearing a fake mask.
Synonyms: Pretence, Showiness, Artificiality
Antonyms: Sincerity, Naturalness, Genuineness, Frankness
Concept Note: Affection is absolute, genuine love; Affectation is a purely theatrical pretense deployed to appear sophisticated to peer groups.

Flaccid (Adjective)

Hindi: ढीला या कमजोर
English: Lacking firmness, crisp elasticity, or operational tone; soft, weak, and loose.
Synonyms: Limp, Slack, Droopy, Sagging, Lax
Antonyms: Firm, Strong, Rigid, Resolute

Contumacy (Noun)

Hindi: अवज्ञा
English: A stubborn, highly arrogant refusal to bow down, follow orders, or comply with institutional authority and rules.
Synonyms: Defiance, Disobedience, Rebellion, Recalcitrance
Antonyms: Compliance, Submission, Docility, Conformity

Oracular (Adjective)

Hindi: रहस्यमय या दिव्य
English: Mysterious, cryptic, and difficult to parse immediately, but carrying underlying wisdom.
Synonyms: Esoteric, Byzantine, Arcane, Cryptic, Enigmatic, Mystical
Antonyms: Direct, Plain, Transparent, Explicit

Cannibalise (Verb)

Hindi: अपने ही संसाधनों को नुकसान पहुँचाना
English: Weakening or devouring one's own products or internal operational resources to feed another segment.
Synonyms: Undermine, Erode, Encroach upon, Weaken
Antonyms: Strengthen, Bolster, Reinforce, Fortify

Foment (Verb)

Hindi: भड़काना
English: To actively instigate, stir up, or provoke public unrest or violent sentiments.
Synonyms: Instigate, Incite, Incense, Precipitate, Provoke, Trigger, Inflame
Antonyms: Appease, Mollify, Placate, Assuage, Lull, Pacify

Lugubrious (Adjective)

Hindi: शोककुल
English: Sounding, looking, or feeling intensely sorrowful, melancholic, or gloomy.
Synonyms: Sad, Mournful, Doleful, Melancholic, Lacrimose, Saturnine
Antonyms: Exuberant, Elated, Ecstatic, Euphoric, Jubilant, Buoyant

Rasping (Adjective)

Hindi: कानों में चुभने वाली आवाज़
English: Harsh, discordant, grating, and deeply irritating to human ears.
Synonyms: Strident, Cacophonous, Clamorous, Shrill, Piercing, Screeching
Antonyms: Mellow, Melodious, Mellifluous, Harmonious

The Consumer & Consumption Taxonomy (OWS)

Teetotaller (Noun)

English: An individual profile who strictly and deliberately refines their lifestyle by never taking alcoholic drinks.
Hindi: मद्यत्यागी.

Somnambulist (Noun)

English: A person dealing with a sleep layout anomaly that causes them to walk around while asleep.
Hindi: नींद में चलने वाला.

Pachyderm (Noun)

English: Large mammals exhibiting highly thick, heavily layered skins (e.g., Rhinoceros, Elephant).
Hindi: मोटी खाल का जानवर.

Cannibal (Noun)

English: An entity that systematically consumes or feeds directly on its own kind.
Hindi: नरभक्षक.

Carnivore (Noun)

English: Flesh-eating or strictly meat-consuming biological organisms.
Hindi: मांसाहारी.

Omnivore (Noun)

English: An adaptable consumer that easily digests everything (both plants and animal flesh).
Hindi: सर्वाहारी.

Herbivore (Noun)

English: A consumer that strictly feeds on organic plant biology.
Hindi: शाकाहारी.

Parasite (Noun)

English: An organism that lives directly inside or on a host body, feeding off their resources.
Hindi: परजीवी.
Related handwritten terms: Leech (जोंक), Freeloader (मुफ़्तखोर), Moocher (कामचोर), Bloodsucker (खून चूसने वाला)

Phrasal Verbs & Color Idioms

Rein in (Phrasal Verb)

English: Pulling back control; using structural authority to tame or dominate a runaway scenario.
Hindi: लगाम कसना (नियंत्रण करना).

Rule out (Phrasal Verb)

English: To completely scratch off or eliminate an entry option from a logical pool.
Hindi: खारिज करना (बाहर करना).

See through (Phrasal Verb)

English: Continuing to execute and finalize a demanding project despite meeting heavy opposition.
Hindi: अंत तक निभाना (पूरा करना).

Show a red card (Idiom)

English: To abruptly dismiss, sack, or fire an employee from their post line.
Hindi: बर्खास्त करना (नौकरी से निकालना).
Related terms: Lay off (छंटनी), Give a pink slip (बर्खास्तगी पत्र), Give walking papers (नौकरी से निकालने का आदेश)

In the red / In red ink (Idiom)

English: Drowning heavily in severe financial losses or industrial debt.
Hindi: घाटे में होना (कर्ज़ में डूबना).

Out of the red (Idiom)

English: Shifting successfully from loss thresholds into highly sustainable, clear profit margins.
Hindi: घाटे से उबरना.

Systemic Grammar Constraints

1. Prepositional Traps: "Dispose of"

The verb dispose uniquely demands a single "F" preposition when indicating throwing out or discarding items. The double "F" variant is grammatically incorrect:

❌ Incorrect layout

"He needed to dispose off his old junk files."

✅ Correct layout

"He needed to dispose of his old junk files."

2. Number Classifications: "Lacuna" vs. "Summons"

Lacuna (Singular Noun)

An unfilled space, gap, or missing portion in a book or manuscript.

Plural Form: Lacunae (Grammatically valid plural counterpart)

Summons (Singular Noun)

An official order to appear in a law court (a single court call). Note that it concludes with 's' but functions strictly as a singular noun.

Plural Form: Summonses (To map out multiple separate court notifications)

The 10 Core Vocabulary Slides

Tepid (Adjective)

Hindi: फीका या उत्साहहीन
English: Showing little enthusiasm, interest, passion, or warmth; lukewarm and flat.
Synonyms: Lukewarm, Indifferent, Apathetic, Half-hearted
Antonyms: Ardent, Fervid, Fervent, Avid
Mam's Effortless Synonyms: Cursory, Perfunctory, Shallow (Lacking depth), Superficial, Hasty (Done in a hurry)

Flaccid (Adjective)

Hindi: कमजोर या ढीला
English: Loose, soft, or completely lacking firmness or structural elasticity; weak and drooping.
Synonyms: Limp, Slack, Droopy, Sagging, Lax
Antonyms: Firm, Strong, Rigid, Resolute
Mam's Context Note: Often tracks skin that stretches or hangs loosely after extreme weight reduction or spending weeks in a hospital bed.

Tumble (Verb)

Hindi: लुढ़कना या अचानक गिरना
English: To fall down suddenly, roll over, or decline rapidly in value or position.
Synonyms: Fall suddenly, Decline rapidly, Plummet, Topple, Cascade
Antonyms: Ascend, Soar, Escalate
Physical Gymnastics: Somersault (flip completely heels-over-head in the air), Cartwheel (sideward rotary wheel rotation)

Quintessential (Adjective)

Hindi: सर्वोत्कृष्ट या आदर्श
English: Serving as the absolutely perfect, ideal, or classic example of a specific quality or class.
Synonyms: Ideal, Perfect, Classic, Exemplary
Antonyms: Imperfect, Flawed, Defective, Atypical

Culpability (Noun)

Hindi: गुनाह या जवाबदेही
English: Responsibility for a fault, error, or crime; the state of deserving blame or retribution.
Synonyms: Accountability, Liability, Guilt, Blameworthiness
Antonyms: Innocence, Exoneration, Blamelessness
Root word: Culp = Blame/Guilt

Disparate (Adjective)

Hindi: अलग-अलग या असमान
English: Essentially different in kind; entirely distinct, mismatched, or unequal.
Synonyms: Different, Distinct, Divergent, Heterogeneous
Antonyms: Similar, Alike, Comparable, Homogeneous

Queasy (Adjective)

Hindi: बेचैन या बीमार सा
English: Feeling nauseous, uneasy, physically unsettled, or sick to one's stomach.
Synonyms: Nauseous, Sick, Uneasy, Squeamish, Unwell
Antonyms: Healthy, Comfortable, Robust, Salubrious (Highly healthy)

Extraneous (Adjective)

Hindi: अप्रासंगिक या फालतू
English: Irrelevant, unnecessary, or unrelated to the subject being dealt with; extra items.
Synonyms: Irrelevant, Unnecessary, Superfluous, Redundant
Antonyms: Relevant, Important, Essential, Necessary, Indispensable

Dulcet (Adjective)

Hindi: मधुर या सुरीला
English: Exceedingly sweet, soothing, fluid, and pleasant to the ears.
Synonyms: Mellow, Melodious, Harmonious, Euphonious, Mellifluous
Antonyms: Harsh, Strident, Discordant, Clamorous, Jeering
Mnemonic: "Dil ko set karne wali sound" = Dulcet (settles the heart beautifully)

Mercurial (Adjective)

Hindi: परिवर्तनशील या अस्थिर
English: Subject to sudden, unpredictable, or volatile changes in mood, temperament, or behavior.
Synonyms: Volatile, Fickle, Changeable, Capricious, Erratic, Whimsical, Variable, Mutable, Temperamental
Antonyms: Stable, Consistent, Predictable, Steady
Root words: Mute / Morph = structural change

Homonym Separation: "Discrete" vs. "Discreet"

Mam tracked these spelling variations to clear up high-yield grammar test traps:

Discreet (Adjective)

English: Behaving in a careful, prudent, and cautious manner to keep secrets safe.
Hindi: विचारशील; सावधान.

Discrete (Adjective)

English: Entirely different, distinct, disconnected, or separate from other entities.
Hindi: अलग; पृथक.

Whiteboard Grids & Clusters

1. Adjectives for "Pristine / Blemish-Free"

Blemish-free

Without marks, blemishes, or impurities.

Immaculate

Perfectly clean, neat, or tidy; free from flaws.

Impeccable

In accordance with the highest standards; faultless.

Irreproachable

Beyond criticism; faultless.

Pristine

In its original condition; unspoiled and clean.

Faultless / Flawless

Without any defects, mistakes, or blemishes.

Untarnished

Not damaged, spoiled, or stained.

Unblemished

Not damaged or marked in any way; pristine.

Unsmeared / Unsullied

Not dirtied, compromised, or made impure.

Exemplary

Serving as a desirable model; representing the best of its kind.

2. Master Cluster for "Carefully Cautious"

Punctilious

Showing great attention to detail or correct behavior.

Meticulous

Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise.

Sedulous

Showing dedication and diligence.

Conscientious

Wishing to do what is right, especially to do one's work or duty well.

Scrupulous

Diligent, thorough, and extremely attentive to details.

Prudent / Circumspect

Acting with or showing care and thought for the future; cautious.

Chary / Wary

Cautiously reluctant to do something; feeling or showing caution.

Heedful / Cautious

Aware of and attentive to; avoiding potential problems.

Vigilant

Keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties.

3. Roots of Culpability (Root: Culp = Blame)

Culprit (Noun)

An individual legally found guilty of an entry error or wrong-doing. (Spelling Trap!)

Exculpate (Verb)

To legally clean or free someone from a frame of blame.

Inculpate (Verb)

To actively charge, frame, or accuse an individual of a crime.

Culpable (Adjective)

Behavior or actions that deserve a heavy dose of blame.

Grammar Alert: "Loose" vs. "Lose"

Mam highlighted a regular spelling and parts-of-speech test trap:

Lose (Verb)

English: To be deprived of or cease to have or retain something.
Hindi: खोना; हारना.
Example: "Do not lose your focus."

Loose (Adjective)

English: Not firmly or tightly fixed in place; detached or slack (flaccid).
Hindi: ढीला.
Example: "A loose tooth / loose clothes."

The 10 Core Vocabulary Slides

Invidious (Adjective)

Hindi: भेदभावपूर्ण या अप्रिय
English: Likely to cause resentment, anger, or jealousy because it is unfair or discriminatory.
Synonyms: Discriminatory, Resentful, Prejudicial, Biased, Partial
Antonyms: Equitable, Impartial, Fair, Just

Perjure (Verb)

Hindi: शपथ तोड़ना या झूठी गवाही देना
English: To deliberately tell a lie in a court of law after taking a solemn oath to tell the truth.
Synonyms: Falsify, Deceive, Lie under oath
Antonyms: Verify, Confirm, Attest
Alternate Forms:
• Perjury (Noun): The criminal act of lying under oath.
• Alibi (Noun): A claim/evidence that one was elsewhere when a crime occurred.

Extol (Verb)

Hindi: भर-भर के तारीफ करना
English: To praise someone or something enthusiastically, highly, and publicly.
Synonyms: Eulogize, Laud, Glorify, Exalt, Appreciate, Applaud, Commend
Antonyms: Denounce, Vilify, Condemn, Criticize

Affectation (Noun)

Hindi: दिखावा या बनावटीपन
English: Artificial or unnatural behavior, speech, or writing that is adopted to impress others; wearing a fake social mask.
Synonyms: Pretence, Showiness, Artificiality
Antonyms: Sincerity, Naturalness, Genuineness, Frankness
Mnemonic Note: Affection is genuine, deep love; Affectation is artificial, fake love to look sophisticated.

Woebegone (Adjective)

Hindi: दुखी या व्यथित
English: Looking or feeling sad, miserable, downcast, or burdened with grief.
Synonyms: Miserable, Unhappy, Forlorn, Melancholic, Lugubrious, Saturnine, Crestfallen, Despondent, Dejected, Doleful, Glum, Gloomy
Antonyms: Jubilant, Elated, Exuberant, Happy, Upbeat

Adulation (Noun)

Hindi: चापलूसी या अति-प्रशंसा
English: Excessive praise, sycophancy, or obsequious flattery.
Synonyms: Flattery, Praise, Worship
Antonyms: Criticism, Condemnation, Disapproval

Querulous (Adjective)

Hindi: शिकायती या चिड़चिड़ा
English: Complaining in a petulant, whining, or irritable manner; habitually finding fault.
Synonyms: Petulant, Captious, Grumbling, Peevish, Whiny
Antonyms: Contented, Satisfied, Complacent, Cheerful

Desultory (Adjective)

Hindi: अव्यवस्थित या बिना उद्देश्य के
English: Lacking a definite plan, purpose, enthusiasm, or system; jumping randomly from one thing to another.
Synonyms: Haphazard, Random, Unsystematic, Aimless
Antonyms: Organized, Methodical, Focused, Disciplined

Rumpus (Noun)

Hindi: हंगामा या शोरशराबा
English: A noisy disturbance, uproar, row, or commotion caused by an argument or fight.
Synonyms: Commotion, Uproar, Pandemonium, Tumultuous, Turbulence, Upheaval, Din, Turmoil
Antonyms: Tranquility, Serenity, Composure, Equanimity

Vilify (Verb)

Hindi: निंदा करना या बदनाम करना
English: To speak or write about someone in a highly insulting, defamatory, or abusive manner.
Synonyms: Defame, Slander, Malign, Libel
Antonyms: Compliment, Praise, Commend

Homonym Breakdown: "Fair" vs. "Fare"

Mam broke down these confusing homonyms to clear up potential exam errors:

Fare (Noun)

English: The money paid to travel on a bus, train, taxi, or plane.
Hindi: किराया; भाड़ा.

Fare (Verb)

English: How well or badly one performs or executes a task. (e.g., "How did you fare in your exams?")
Hindi: प्रदर्शन करना; प्रगति करना.

Fair (Adjective Form 1)

English: Just, unbiased, and completely impartial.
Hindi: निष्पक्ष; उचित.

Fair (Adjective Form 2)

English: Light complexioned or pale skinned.
Hindi: गोरा; साफ.

Fair (Adjective Form 3)

English: Good, fine, or pleasant (e.g., fair weather).
Hindi: सुहावना; अच्छा.

Fair (Noun)

English: A gathering of stalls and amusements for public entertainment; a carnival.
Hindi: मेला; प्रदर्शनी.

One-Word Substitutions (OWS)

Graffiti (Noun)

English: Writing or drawings scribbled, scratched, or sprayed illicitly on a wall or other public surface.
Hindi: दीवारों पर बनाई गई चित्रकारी/लिखावट.

Claustrophobia (Noun)

English: An extreme, highly irrational psychological fear of being confined in small, tight, or enclosed locations.
Hindi: बंद स्थानों का अत्यधिक भय.

Agoraphobia (Noun)

English: An extreme, irrational fear of entering open, vast, or heavily crowded public spaces.
Hindi: खुले या भीड़भाड़ वाले स्थानों का भय.

Iconoclast (Noun)

English: A person who attacks or rejects cherished traditional beliefs, icons, or long-established social institutions.
Hindi: रूढ़िभंजक (परंपराओं को तोड़ने वाला).
Peer term: Heretic (धर्मद्रोही/नास्तिक).

Horde (Noun)

English: A massive, loosely structured, or wandering group of people gathering simultaneously.
Hindi: झुंड; लोगों का बड़ा समूह.

The Grammar Unit

Linguistic Constraints of Cognitive State Verbs: "To Know"

Mam mapped out an absolute baseline rule regarding state verbs: Verbs like Know represent cognitive states and cannot be used in Continuous or Present Perfect Continuous structural tenses.

❌ Incorrect continuous structure

"I have been knowing him for ten years."

✅ Correct perfect layout

"I have known him for ten years."

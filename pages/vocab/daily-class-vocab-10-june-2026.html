The 10 Core Vocabulary Slides

Tenacity (Noun)

Hindi: दृढ़ता या जिद
English: The quality of holding on firmly to a goal or value, refusing to give up regardless of operational difficulties.
Synonyms: Perseverance, Doggedness, Resoluteness
Antonyms: Frailty, Weakness, Fickleness, Instability
Tenacious Adjectives: Steadfast, Resolute, Determined, Relentless, Unyielding, Unceasing, Incessant, Unwavering, Unswerving, Persistent, Dogged, Persevering

Exhilaration (Noun)

Hindi: आनंद या अत्यधिक खुशी
English: A powerful feeling of intense excitement, energy, and happiness.
Synonyms: Delight, Jubilation, Exultation, Ecstasy, Rapture
Antonyms: Depression, Misery, Gloom, Sorrow, Grief
Mam's Core Additions: Glee, Euphoria, Bliss

Depraved (Adjective)

Hindi: भ्रष्ट या दुष्ट

Synonyms: Wicked, Evil, Degenerate, Debauched, Corrupt
Antonyms: Virtuous, Upright, Righteous, Ethical

Sentient (Adjective)

Synonyms: Feeling, Experiencing, Conscious, Aware
Antonyms: Unaware, Oblivious, Ignorant, Unconscious

Uproot (Verb)

The Grammar Unit

The Structural Execution of Material Clearances: "Dispose of"

Mam mapped out this exact error baseline to clear up regular test traps regarding disposing of items:

❌ Incorrect double 'F' variant

"He decided to dispose off his old junk application files."

✅ Correct structural variant

"He decided to dispose of his old junk application files."

Rule: The verb dispose takes a single "F" preposition (of), never the double "F" variant (off), when denoting the discarding of items.

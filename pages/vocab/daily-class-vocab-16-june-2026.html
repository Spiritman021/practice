Core 10 Vocabulary Words & Handwritten Synonyms

Fecund (Adjective)

Hindi: उपजाऊ या फलदायी
English: Highly productive; fertile; producing many ideas or offspring.
Synonyms: Prolific, Fertile, Generative
Antonyms: Barren, Sterile, Unproductive
Mam's Extra Word: Ground-breaking (Innovative)

Spree (Noun)

Hindi: मौज-मस्ती या अनियंत्रित गतिविधि
English: A period of unconstrained activity, enjoyment, or spending.
Synonyms: Binge, Revelry, Frolic
Antonyms: Control, Moderation, Discipline
Mam's Extra Notes:
• Revel (Verb): To enjoy.
• Carnival (Noun): A festival or fair. (Root: Carni means Flesh/Meat — historically, people ate meat during festivals).
• Gala (Noun): A celebratory festival or big public event.
• Pageant (Noun): An exciting public show/event.
• Fete (Noun): Festival/celebration.
• Extravaganza (Noun): A large, spectacular show with a lot of show-off.

Adroit (Adjective)

Hindi: चतुर या निपुण
English: Clever, skillful, and adept in handling situations.
Synonyms: Clever, Shrewd, Canny, Discerning, Sagacious, Competent, Potent, Ingenious
Antonyms: Clumsy, Inept, Incompetent, Bungling (Messing up constantly)
Important Term: Amateur (A person who does something as a hobby/enjoyment, not as a profession).

Tepid (Adjective)

Hindi: गुनगुना या उत्साहहीन
English: Slightly warm (lukewarm); showing little enthusiasm.
Synonyms: Lukewarm, Unenthusiastic
Antonyms: Hot, Passionate
Mam's Extra Synonyms (Zeal): Ardent, Avid, Fervent, Fervid

Rebuff (Verb)

Hindi: अस्वीकार करना या दुत्कारना
English: To reject or refuse something bluntly or unceremoniously.
Synonyms: Repudiate, Spurn, Dismiss, Snub
Antonyms: Accept, Welcome, Embrace, Adopt
Phrasal Verbs: Turn down, Brush off
Extra Word: Viable (Practical).

Virulent (Adjective)

Hindi: जहरीला या अत्यंत हानिकारक
English: Extremely harmful, poisonous, or bitterly hostile.
Synonyms: Malignant, Toxic, Hostile, Noxious, Jeopardous, Pernicious, Deadly
Antonyms: Harmless, Benign, Innocuous, Amiable (Friendly)

Salubrious (Adjective)

Hindi: स्वास्थ्यवर्धक या सुखद
English: Healthy, pleasant, and beneficial to well-being.
Synonyms: Wholesome, Salutary, Invigorating
Antonyms: Noxious, Unhealthful, Deleterious
Mnemonic Trick: "Sallu Bhai (Salman Khan) will stay healthy if he brushes daily."
Three 'R' Words (Nouns meaning Relief/Rest): Respite, Reprieve, Relief.

Jester (Noun)

Hindi: विदूषक या मसखरा
English: A professional clown or comedian employed to entertain people.
Synonyms: Clown, Joker, Comedian
Antonyms: Serious person, Scholar, Thinker, Intellectual
Note: Foreigners often use "Clown" in a negative/mocking sense to insult someone's intelligence.

Tendentious (Adjective)

Hindi: पक्षपातपूर्ण
English: Expressing or promoting a particular, biased cause or point of view.
Synonyms: Partisan, Prejudicial, Polemical, Skewed (Biased/One-sided)
Antonyms: Objective, Impartial, Dispassionate, Neutral

Insular (Adjective)

Hindi: संकीर्ण विचारधारा वाला
English: Narrow-minded; isolated from outside cultures or ideas.
Synonyms: Narrow-minded, Isolated, Provincial, Parochial
Antonyms: Broad-minded, Open-minded, Cosmopolitan, Worldly
Analogy: Kuan ka mendhak (कुएं का मेंढक - Frog in a well) / Living in a bubble.

🐾 Mam's Handwritten Animal-Like Adjectives

These adjectives end in the suffix -ine (except Avian) to describe animal-like qualities:

Feline

Cat-like; resembling a cat.

Canine

Dog-like; resembling a dog.

Equine

Horse-like; resembling a horse.

Bovine

Cow-like; slow/dull resembling a cow.

Piscine

Fish-like; relating to fish.

Vulpine

Fox-like; describing a very cunning or clever person.

Porcine

Pig-like; resembling a pig.

Ovine

Sheep-like; resembling a sheep.

Lupine

Wolf-like; savage or fierce like a wolf.

Avian

Related to birds (exception to -ine suffix).

One Word Substitutions (OWS)

Bereft (Adjective)

English: Feeling sad and lonely due to someone's death or departure; deprived of something.
Hindi: वंचित; शोक-संतप्त.

Bungle (Verb/Noun)

English: To carry out a task clumsily or incompetently; messing up a situation completely.
Hindi: गड़बड़ करना; भूल करना.

Benefactor (Noun)

English: A person who gives money or other help to a person or cause.
Hindi: दानदाता; धर्मोपकारी.

Bottleneck (Noun)

English: A situation that causes delay in a process or blocks progress; congestion/blockage.
Hindi: बाधा; संकीर्ण मार्ग.

Phrasal Verbs (PV)

Throw out (Phrasal Verb)

English: To discard or get rid of something.
Hindi: बाहर फेंक देना; निपटाना (Dispose of).

Throw up (Phrasal Verb)

English: To vomit; eject from stomach.
Hindi: उल्टी करना.

Tie up (Phrasal Verb)

English: To bind or fasten something very tightly.
Hindi: बाँधना; व्यस्त रखना.

Tire out (Phrasal Verb)

English: To exhaust someone completely.
Hindi: थका देना (Enervate).

Idioms & Phrases (I&P)

1. Adroit / Skill Category

Know the ropes

To be experienced and know how to do a job properly.

Have a knack for

To possess a natural talent or skill for something.

Green thumb

Natural talent for gardening and growing plants.

A cut above the rest

Superior; noticeably better than the remaining group.

2. Health Category

In the pink of health

In excellent physical condition and health.

Fit as a fiddle

Completely healthy and physically fit.

Hail and hearty

Healthy and strong.

Alive and kicking

Active, healthy, and full of energy.

3. Laughter & Bias Categories

ROFL

Rolling on the floor laughing; laughing uncontrollably.

In stitches

Laughing so hard that your stomach hurts as if it needs medical stitches.

Tilt the scales

To make a situation biased or favor one side over another.

Slant/Skew the facts

To alter data or facts to represent a one-sided, biased narrative.

4. Slide / Revision Idioms

Cinderella story

A "rags-to-riches" journey where someone achieves extreme success starting from poverty.

Lace up your shoes

To prepare yourself to take action; get ready to work hard.

If the shoe fits, wear it

If a critical remark or criticism applies to you, you should accept it.

Walk in the shoes of greatness

To live or act like highly respected, great individuals.

Make shoes for a long journey

Preparing oneself thoroughly for a difficult or prolonged trial.

Too big for your shoes/boots

Becoming highly arrogant, conceited, or snobbish (Snooty).

🌍 Geopolitical & Science Terminology

Convergence (Physics/Strategy)

Meeting or coming together at a focal point (like light rays through a Convex Lens).

Diversion / Divergence (Physics/Strategy)

Moving apart or separating in different directions (like light rays reflecting out of a Concave Lens).

Strategic Autonomy (Geopolitics)

The ability of a nation to pursue its own national interests and execute foreign policy choices independently without being pressured by stronger countries.

Multi-polar World Order (Geopolitics)

A global system where political, economic, and military power is distributed among multiple strong nations (like India, US, China, Russia) rather than concentrated in a single place.

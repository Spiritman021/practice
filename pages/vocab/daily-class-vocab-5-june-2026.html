The 10 Core Vocabulary Slides

Churn (Noun / Verb)

Hindi: मथना (v) / बदलाव, अशांति (n)
English: As a verb, to move something rapidly and violently. As a noun, a massive, turbulent shift, disturbance, or structural change.
Synonyms: Upheaval, Turbulence, Metamorphosis
Antonyms: Stability, Equilibrium, Continuity
Root Word Note: Morph / Mute indicate modification, change, or transformation (e.g., Metamorphosis, Mutate).

Impute (Verb)

Hindi: इल्जाम लगाना; मढ़ना
English: To point out the source or cause of something bad; to assign blame or responsibility to someone.
Synonyms: Attribute, Ascribe, Accuse
Antonyms: Exonerate, Acquit, Clear
Core Additions: Bleam, Allege, Charge
Linguistic Contrast: Inculpate (to assign guilt/charge) vs. Exculpate (to free/vindicate from blame).

Inexorable (Adjective)

Hindi: जिसे रोका न जा सके; कठोर
English: An ongoing momentum or action that is absolutely impossible to stop, prevent, alter, or persuade.
Synonyms: Relentless, Unyielding, Implacable
Antonyms: Flexible, Yielding, Amenable, Pliable, Compliant, Adherent

Quixotic (Adjective) ⭐🌟 High-Yield

Hindi: काल्पनिक; अव्यावहारिक
English: Having lofty, grand, or romantic ideas that are highly unrealistic, impractical, and decoupled from everyday reality.
Synonyms: Impractical, Idealistic, Unrealistic
Antonyms: Practical, Realistic, Sensible, Pragmatic, Rational
Note: "Rational" represents highly logical thinking.

Diffident (Adjective)

Hindi: शर्मीला; आत्मसंशय से भरा
English: Lacking self-confidence; deeply shy, timid, or hesitant to voice opinions.
Synonyms: Timorous, Timid, Trepid, Reticent, Self-effacing
Antonyms: Self-assured, Audacious, Confident, Bold
Additions: Laconic, Taciturn, Bashful, Coy
Speech Variant: Eloquent (expressing ideas beautifully and fluently).

Licentious (Adjective)

Hindi: अनैतिक; भ्रष्ट; कामुक
English: Lacking moral restraint, values, or ethical discipline; behaving in a corrupt or unprincipled way.
Synonyms: Immoral, Dissolute, Debauched
Antonyms: Moral, Virtuous, Decent, Noble
Additions: Wanton, Wretched

Truncate (Verb)

Hindi: काटकर छोटा करना
English: To cut off, shorten, or reduce the length or duration of something by lopping off parts.
Synonyms: Abbreviate, Curtail, Abridge, Condense
Antonyms: Extend, Elongate, Amplify
Additions: Prune (trees/leaves), Trim (hair), Pare down, Abate, Ebb

Contumacy (Noun)

Hindi: अवज्ञा; हठ; ढिठाई
English: Stubborn, arrogant refusal to comply with authority, instructions, or rules.
Synonyms: Defiance, Disobedience, Rebellion, Recalcitrance
Antonyms: Compliance, Submission, Docility, Conformity
Additions: Resistance, Opposition
Linguistic Variant: Mutiny (active military/sepoys revolt against orders).

Lacuna (Noun)

Hindi: कमी; अंतराल; खाली जगह
English: An empty space, structural gap, missing link, or deficiency in something.
Synonyms: Gap, Deficiency, Missing part, Void
Antonyms: Completeness, Sufficiency, Fulfillment
Additions: Hiatis
Grammar Alert: Singular: Lacuna | Plural: Lacunae. (Error: "These lacuna" is invalid; use "These lacunae").

Antediluvian (Adjective)

Hindi: अत्यंत प्राचीन; पुराना
English: Completely obsolete, archaic, or old-fashioned; tracking back to ancient times.
Synonyms: Ancient, Archaic, Primitive, Outdated
Antonyms: Modern, Contemporary, Current
Additions: Outmoded, Obsolete
Double-Meaning: Contemporary can mean modern OR existing at the exact same timeframe.

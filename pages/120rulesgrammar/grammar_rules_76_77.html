at = point (smallest) · on = surface/day/date (medium) · in = area/volume/month/year (largest) · yesterday/today/tomorrow → NO preposition

📋 Rule 76 — Quick Recap

  • at = point / smallest: clock time, small place: "at 4 o'clock" · "at lunch time" · "at night" · "at Laxmi Nagar"
  • on = surface / medium: day, date: "on Monday" · "on 15th August" · "on 30th May"
  • in = area/volume / largest: month, year, large place: "in April" · "in 2009" · "in Delhi" · "in the morning"
  • morning/evening = in (welcoming) · night = at (departure)
  • NO preposition with: yesterday · today · tomorrow · last Monday · coming Sunday · last night
77

both…and (NEVER "both…as well as") · good at (NEVER "good in")

"both" is ALWAYS followed by "and" — never "as well as", never "not", never "also" · "good" is ALWAYS followed by "at" — never "in"

→ Part 1: both…and (NEVER both…as well as)
★ Rule 77 (Part 1): "both" is ALWAYS followed by "and". Never use "as well as", "not", or "also" after "both".
both + [noun/adj] + and + [noun/adj] ✓
NEVER: "both…as well as" ✗ · "both…not" ✗ · "both…also" ✗
✗
Both Ram as well as Shyam are good at English. [Incorrect]
"both...as well as" → WRONG. ✗
✓
Both Ram and Shyam are good at English. [Correct]
"both...and" → correct. ✓
✗
Both men as well as women have complained. [Incorrect]
✓
Both men and women have complained. [Correct]
✓
I felt both happy and sad at the same time. [Correct]
"both happy and sad" → "both...and" used correctly. ✓
✓
She is both intelligent and hardworking. [Correct]
"both...and" → ✓
★ Part 2: good AT (NEVER good in)
⭐
STAR MARK this rule: "good" is ALWAYS followed by the preposition "at". NEVER use "in" after "good". This is one of the most common spoken English mistakes!
good at something ✓  ·  good in something ✗
"I am good in English" ✗ → "I am good at English" ✓
✗
I am good in English. [Incorrect]
"good in" NEVER used. ✗
✓
I am very good at English. [Correct]
"good at" → always correct. ✓
✗
She is good in geography. [Incorrect]
✓
She is very good at geography. [Correct]
✗
Rohan is good in football. [Incorrect]
✓
Rohan is good at football. [Correct]
💡
Memory Trick:
We say "good at" because "at" = a point/skill — you are pointing to a specific skill.
"She is good at geography" = geography is the specific skill she excels at (a point of expertise).

Common mistake to avoid every single day: "I am good in English" ✗ → "I am good at English" ✓

📋 Rule 77 — Quick Recap

  • "both" → always followed by "and": "Both Ram and Shyam…" ✓ · "Both men and women…" ✓
  • NEVER: "both…as well as" ✗ · "both…also" ✗ · "both…not" ✗
  • "I felt both happy and sad at the same time." ✓
  • "good" → always followed by "at": "good at English" ✓ · "good in English" ✗
  • "She is very good at geography." ✓ · "Rohan is good at football." ✓ · "I am good at English." ✓

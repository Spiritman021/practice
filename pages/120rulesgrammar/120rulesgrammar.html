Topic 11: Rules 31 to 35

Topic 12: Rules 36, 37 & 38

Comparing two qualities of the same object — always use "more" + Positive Degree, never Comparative Degree (Rule 36). "more than one" and "many a" take singular noun + singular verb; "many", "a great many", "a good many" take plural noun + plural verb (Rule 37). "doubt/doubtful" takes connector "if/whether"; "no doubt/not doubtful" takes connector "that" (Rule 38).

Topic 13: Rules 39 & 40

Narration — Sequence of Tenses: main clause past → subordinate also past (will→would, can→could, may→might), with 4 exceptions where tense stays Simple Present: Universal Truth, Historical Facts, Proverbs, Habitual Facts — including the famous "honey is sweet" vs "the honey was sweet" distinction (Rule 39). Use of little / a little / the little with uncountable nouns and few / a few / the few with countable nouns (negative / positive / neutral meanings), plus some vs any (Rule 40).

Topic 14: Rules 41, 42 & 43

Objective case of pronoun always comes after a preposition or verb — never subjective (Rule 41). Order of pronouns: positive sense → 2-3-1 (You, He, I); negative sense / mistakes → 1-2-3 (I, You, He); all plural pronouns → always 1-2-3 (Rule 42). Superfluous errors — 15+ redundant phrases like "previous records", "wet water", "suppose if", "retreated back", "reborn again", "mutual agreement", "forbade not", "passing marks" etc. (Rule 43).

Topic 15: Rules 44, 45 & 46

Use of Apostrophe ('s): shows possession (Shyam's car = Car of Shyam), hissing sound rule (Manas' car), NOT with non-living things (legs of the table ✓), 8 exceptions (time, weight, distance, place, idiom, unit, heavenly body, personification), and NEVER with possessive pronouns (yours/ours/its) (Rule 44). Past Point of Time (yesterday/last week/ago) → always Simple Past tense (Rule 45). Absolute words like unique/excellent/perfect/ideal/impossible that are NEVER used with "most" or "more" — full 24-word table (Rule 46).

Topic 16: Rules 47–50 🏆 Half Century!

Quite vs Quiet (Q-U-I-E-T = silent noun · Q-U-I-T-E = completely adverb) and "quite all" is always wrong — superfluous (Rule 47). Ago = one event → Simple Past · Before = two events → earlier event takes Past Perfect (had+V3), later takes Simple Past (Rule 48). Common Possession (apostrophe at last noun only) vs Split Possession (apostrophe at each noun → must use plural noun) (Rule 49). it's = "it is"/"it has" vs its = possessive adjective — with quick substitution test (Rule 50).

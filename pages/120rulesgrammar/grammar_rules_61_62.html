61

"a" vs "an" — Always Determined by SOUND, Not by Spelling

Vowel SOUND → "an" · Consonant SOUND → "a" · Words starting with y / w → always "a" (exception)

→ The Core Rule
★ From the book (Rule 61): The choice between the two indefinite articles – 'a' & 'an' – is determined by sound. Words beginning with consonant sounds are preceded by 'a' and words beginning with vowel sounds are preceded by 'an'.

Vowels – a, e, i, o, and u  ·  Consonants – b, c, d, f, … z
Vowel SOUND (अ से अं) → an  ·  Consonant SOUND (क से ज्ञ) → a
⚠️ NEVER look at the letter — always listen to the SOUND
💡
Why "a university" and NOT "an university"?
"university" starts with the letter "u" (a vowel) — but it sounds like "yuniversity" — and "y" is a consonant sound. So → a university. ✓

"NCC" starts with "N" (a consonant letter) — but it sounds like "en-CC" — and "en" is a vowel sound. So → an NCC cadet. ✓
Vowel SOUND → "an"
an NCC cadet an umbrella an M.Tech. graduate an honest man an hour an MBA
✓
An NCC cadet. [Vowel sounding word — "en"-CC]
N sounds like "en" → vowel sound → "an". ✓
✓
An umbrella. [Vowel sounding word — "ʌ"]
Starts with vowel sound "ʌ" → "an". ✓
✓
An M. Tech. graduate. [Vowel sounding word — "em"]
M sounds like "em" → vowel sound → "an". ✓
Consonant SOUND → "a"
a UNICEF nation a utensil a university a unicorn a B.Tech. candidate a unique pen
✓
A UNICEF participating nation. [Consonant sounding word — "yu"]
UNICEF starts with "yu" sound → consonant sound → "a". ✓
✓
A utensil. [Consonant sounding word — "yu"]
"utensil" sounds like "yu-tensil" → "a". ✓
✓
A university. [Consonant sounding word — "yu"]
"university" sounds like "yu-niversity" → "a". ✓
✓
A unicorn. [Consonant sounding word — "yu"]
"unicorn" sounds like "yu-nicorn" → "a". ✓
★ Exception — Words Starting with "y" or "w" → Always "a"
⭐
From the book (highlighted rule): With every word that starts with "y" and "w" we use article "a" — ALWAYS.
These letters produce consonant sounds, so "an" is never used.
✓
A year. [exception — starts with "y" → always "a"]
"year" starts with "y" (consonant sound) → "a". ✓
✓
A woman. [starts with "w" → always "a"]
"w" → consonant sound → "a". ✓
✓
A European university. ["yu" sound at start]
"European" sounds like "yu-ropean" → consonant sound → "a". ✓
All book examples — complete list
a year a European university a University a Uniform a one-rupee note a Union a one-eyed girl a unanimous decision
Note on "one": "one" starts with the letter "o" (vowel) but sounds like "wʌn" → starts with "w" sound → consonant sound → "a one-rupee note" ✓ · "an one-rupee note" ✗

📋 Rule 61 — Quick Recap

  • Choice of a/an depends on SOUND, not the spelling of the letter
  • Vowel SOUND → "an": "an NCC" ✓ · "an umbrella" ✓ · "an M.Tech" ✓ · "an honest man" ✓
  • Consonant SOUND → "a": "a university" ✓ · "a UNICEF" ✓ · "a unique pen" ✓ · "a utensil" ✓
  • Words starting with y / w → always "a": "a year" ✓ · "a woman" ✓ · "a one-rupee note" ✓ (one sounds like "wun")

Definite Article "The" — 9 Complete Cases

Unique nouns · Newspapers · Countries (abbreviated) · Superlative · Comparative (2 cases) · Metaphor · Proper+Common noun · Celestial bodies · Government orgs

→ All 9 Cases — Overview
★ From the book (Rule 62): Usage of definite ARTICLE "The" — 9 cases where "the" is always used.
(1)
Unique Nouns
Mountains, Bays, Deserts, Rivers, Oceans, Seas, Musical instruments, Religious books, Canals, Ships, Political parties…
(2)
Newspapers
The Times of India · The Hindu · The Indian Express
(3)
Countries (abbreviated)
The USA · The UK · The UAE · The USSR · The Netherlands · The Maldives · The Philippines
(4)
Superlative Degree
The best book · The darkest cloud · The most beautiful girl
(5)
Comparative Degree (2 cases)
Case 1: "The higher you go, the cooler it is." · Case 2: "He is the better of the two."
(6)
Metaphor
He is the Mahatma Gandhi of our class. · Delhi is the Paris of India.
(7)
Common + Proper Noun Combo
The river Nile · The New York City · The Muslim League Party
(8)
Celestial Bodies
The moon · The earth · The sun · The sky · The planets
(9)
Government Organisations
The judiciary · The WTO · The RBI · The Supreme Court · The UNICEF
(1) Unique Nouns — Mountains, Oceans, Rivers, Deserts, Musical Instruments…
★ From the book: Series of Mountains, Bays/Gulfs, Group of islands, Canals, Aeroplanes, Ships, Planes, Religious books, Government branches, Armed forces, Names of Deserts, Political parties, Musical instruments, Rivers, Oceans, Seas, and things which are "unique" in themselves: These nouns are always preceded by a definite article "The".
✓
The Sahara. [Name of Desert]
✓
The Pacific. [Name of Ocean]
✓
The Nile. [Name of River]
✓
The Coral Sea. [Name of Sea]
✓
The Himalayas. [Name of Mountain Range] ✓
✓
The Guitar. [Musical Instrument]
✓
The Bay of Bengal · The Indian Ocean · The Arabian Sea · The Ganga · The Yamuna. ✓
⭐
★ Very Important — Mountain RANGE vs Mountain PEAK:
Mountain RANGE → "The" is used: "The Himalayas" ✓
Mountain PEAK → "The" is NEVER used: "Mount Everest" ✓ · "Mount Kilimanjaro" ✓
✗ "The Mount Everest" is WRONG. We only say "Everest" or "Mount Everest".
✓
The Himalayas [Mountain Range — "the"]
✗
The Mount Everest [Mountain Peak — NO "the"]
(2) Newspapers · (3) Countries (Abbreviated)
Newspapers — always "The"
✓
The Times of India · The Hindu · The Indian Express
All newspaper names → always preceded by "The". ✓
Countries (abbreviated / union-based) → "The"
★ From the book: Names of Countries (Only the ones which are written in Abbreviated forms): These nouns are always preceded by a definite article "The". Also countries formed from union of states/regions.
✓
The USA · The UK · The UAE · The USSR · The Netherlands · The Maldives · The Philippines
Abbreviated/union-based countries → always "The". ✓
✗
The India · The China · The Japan · The Australia — All INCORRECT
Non-abbreviated country names → NO "The". India, China, Japan, Australia never take "the". ✗
💡
Why do some countries take "the"?
Countries formed by uniting separate states (e.g., the United States, the United Kingdom, the United Arab Emirates) take "the" because historically they were a union/group. Other simple country names (India, Japan, China) do not.
(4) Superlative Degree · (5) Comparative Degree
Superlative Degree → always "The"
✓
The best book. [Superlative]
✓
The darkest cloud. [Superlative]
✓
She is the most beautiful girl of my class. [Superlative]
✓
You are the most talented students of the world. [Superlative]
Comparative Degree — 2 specific cases
CASE 1 — "The more…the more" pattern

Both comparative adjectives/adverbs get "the" — "The ___er you ___, the ___er it ___"

✓
The harder you work, the better results you get.
Both comparative forms → "the". ✓
✓
The faster you walk, the sooner you reach.
✓
The higher you go, the cooler it is.
✓
The more sugar you put in the dish, the sweeter it is.
✓
The more classes you attend, the better your understanding.
CASE 2 — Selecting one out of two

When comparing TWO things and selecting ONE → comparative + "the"

✓
He is the better of the two.
Comparing two people → "the better of the two". ✓
✓
She is the poorer of the two women.
✓
He is the better candidate of the two for the job.
(6) Metaphor · (7) Common + Proper Noun Combo · (8) Celestial Bodies · (9) Government Orgs
Metaphor — "The" before the comparison
★ From the book (6): Sometimes "the" is used before a metaphor. In case of a metaphor one thing is treated as another thing.
✓
He is the Mahatma Gandhi of our class.
He is being compared TO Mahatma Gandhi (metaphor) → "the". ✓
✓
Delhi is the Paris of India.
Delhi is compared TO Paris → "the". ✓
✓
Kalidasa is the Shakespeare of India.
✓
Kashmir is the Switzerland of India.
✓
She is the Mother Teresa of our class.
(7) Common noun + Proper noun combination
★ From the book (7): For a phrase which is combination of a common and proper noun, 'the' is used.
✓
The river Nile. ["river" (common) + "Nile" (proper) → "The"]
✓
The New York City. ["City" (common) + "New York" (proper) → "The"]
✓
The Muslim League Party. ["Party" (common) + "Muslim League" (proper) → "The"]
(8) Celestial Bodies — one of a kind in the universe
★ From the book (8): 'The' is mandatory before a thing which is only one of a kind in the universe.
✓
The moon is shining tonight.
✓
The earth is moving around the sun.
✓
The sun rises in the east.
(9) Government Organisations
★ From the book (9): For the names of the government organizations, 'the' is used.
✓
The judiciary · The WTO · The RBI · The Supreme Court · The UNICEF
✓
The Society for the protection of Child's Rights.
✓
The judiciary is composed of a supreme court…

📋 Rule 62 — "The" Quick Recap (9 Cases)

  • (1) Unique nouns: mountains, oceans, rivers, deserts, seas, musical instruments, canals, ships, religious books, political parties, armed forces → "The Sahara" · "The Himalayas" · "The Guitar"
  • ⭐ Mountain range → "The Himalayas" ✓ · Mountain peak → "Mount Everest" (no "the") ✓
  • (2) Newspapers: "The Hindu" · "The Times of India" ✓
  • (3) Countries (abbreviated/union): The USA, The UK, The UAE, The USSR, The Netherlands, The Maldives, The Philippines. India/China/Japan → NO "the"
  • (4) Superlative: "The best" · "The darkest" · "The most beautiful" ✓
  • (5) Comparative Case 1: "The harder you work, the better results you get." ✓
         Comparative Case 2: "He is the better of the two." ✓
  • (6) Metaphor: "Delhi is the Paris of India." · "Kalidasa is the Shakespeare of India." ✓
  • (7) Common + Proper noun: "The river Nile" · "The New York City" ✓
  • (8) Celestial bodies: "The moon" · "The earth" · "The sun" ✓
  • (9) Government organisations: "The judiciary" · "The RBI" · "The Supreme Court" ✓

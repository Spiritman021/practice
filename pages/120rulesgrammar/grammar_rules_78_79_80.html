78

"lest" = otherwise (अन्यथा) — Always followed by "should" or bare verb ONLY

lest = अन्यथा / for fear that · After lest → only "should" or no modal · NEVER will/would/can/could/may/might/to

→ What does "lest" mean?
★ Rule 78: "lest" means "otherwise" / "अन्यथा" / "for fear that".
मेहनत कर लो अन्यथा तुम फेल हो जाओगे → "Work hard lest you fail."

After "lest", you can ONLY use:
  1. should → "lest you should fail" ✓
  2. Nothing (bare infinitive / V1) → "lest you fail" ✓
NOTHING else. "lest" does not allow any other modal verb.
✓ Allowed after lest
should bare verb (V1)
✗ NEVER use after lest
will would can could may might to is/am/are
lest + [subject] + should + V1 ✓   OR   lest + [subject] + V1 ✓
lest + will/would/can/could/may/might → ALWAYS WRONG ✗
★ All Examples from Class
✓
Work hard lest you fail. [Correct — bare verb]
lest + bare V1 "fail" ✓
✓
Work hard lest you should fail. [Correct — should]
lest + "should" + V1 ✓
✗
Work hard lest you would fail. [Incorrect]
"would" CANNOT follow "lest". ✗ Only "should" or bare verb allowed.
✓
He removed the remark lest listeners should be offended. [Correct]
lest + "should" + V1 ✓ — "He removed it otherwise listeners would be offended."
✓
Be loyal lest you should face consequences. [Correct]
lest + "should" + V1 ✓
✓
Be very loyal towards God lest you face problems. [Correct]
lest + bare V1 ✓
💡
Memory Trick:
"lest" says: "My life goal is clear — either should or nothing. No one else in my life!"

Think of "lest" as a strict person who only accepts "should" or goes alone (bare verb).
Spot "lest" → write "should" or nothing. Never write will/would/can/may.

📋 Rule 78 — Quick Recap

  • "lest" = otherwise (अन्यथा) / for fear that
  • After lest → ONLY "should" OR bare verb (V1)
  • "Work hard lest you fail." ✓ · "Work hard lest you should fail." ✓
  • NEVER: "lest you would fail" ✗ · "lest you will fail" ✗ · "lest you can fail" ✗
79

until / till (TIME) vs unless (ACTION) · till cannot start a sentence

until/till = time-oriented (जब तक…समय) · unless = action-oriented (जब तक…काम) · till ≠ sentence starter

→ until/till vs unless — Core Difference
until / till
TIME-oriented
Used when talking about TIME.
"जब तक (समय की बात)"

"He waited for you till sunset."
"We ate nothing until midnight."
"Until your next birthday, you will have to wait."
unless
ACTION-oriented
Used when talking about a condition/ACTION.
"जब तक (काम की बात)"

"He will not move unless you give him the keys."
"Taxpayer base won't increase unless the government reduces taxes."
TIME → until / till  ·  ACTION/CONDITION → unless
★ till vs until — The ONE Difference
★ till and until mean the same thing (both = time), BUT there is ONE difference:
"till" CANNOT be used at the START of a sentence.
"until" CAN be used anywhere — beginning, middle, or end.
✗
Till your next birthday, you will wait. [Wrong at start]
"till" cannot start a sentence. ✗
✓
Until your next birthday, you will wait. [Correct at start]
"until" can start a sentence. ✓
✓
We ate nothing till midnight. [Correct — till in middle]
"till" in the middle of sentence → correct. ✓
✓
We ate nothing until midnight. [Correct — until anywhere]
★ All Examples — until/unless
TIME examples → until / till
✓
He waited for you till/until sunset. [Time → till/until ✓]
Sunset = point in time → till or until. ✓
✓
We ate nothing until midnight. [Time → until ✓]
✓
Until the sun and moon remain, I will keep studying. [Time → until ✓]
ACTION examples → unless
✓
He will not move from here unless you give him the keys of your car. [Action → unless ✓]
"giving the keys" is an action/condition → unless. ✓
✓
The taxpayer base will not increase unless the government reduces taxes. [Action → unless ✓]
"reducing taxes" is an action → unless. ✓
💡
Quick Test:
Ask: "Is this about a TIME (clock, event, day)?" → use until/till.
Ask: "Is this about a CONDITION or an ACTION someone must do?" → use unless.

"Wait until 5 pm." ✓ (time) · "I won't go unless you call me." ✓ (action)

📋 Rule 79 — Quick Recap

  • until/till = time-oriented: "He waited till sunset." ✓ · "We ate nothing until midnight." ✓
  • unless = action/condition-oriented: "He won't move unless you give the keys." ✓
  • till vs until: till CANNOT start a sentence · until CAN start anywhere: "Until your birthday, wait." ✓ · "Till your birthday, wait." ✗
  • Both till and until = same meaning (time) · only position restriction on "till"
80

Error of Parallelism — Both Sides of a Connector Must Be the SAME Part of Speech

and · not only…but also · either…or · neither…nor · rather…than · or — all require parallel (matching) structure on both sides

→ What is Parallelism?
★ Rule 80: When certain connectors (and, or, not only…but also, neither…nor, either…or, rather…than) join two parts of a sentence, both parts MUST use the same part of speech and same form. This is called Parallelism.
[Part of Speech A] + connector + [same Part of Speech A] ✓
V1 + and + V1 · adj + and + adj · noun + and + noun · V-ing + and + V-ing · to+V + and + to+V
Connectors that REQUIRE parallelism (compulsory)
and or not only…but also either…or neither…nor rather…than
★ All Examples from Class — Identify the Error
Wrong ✗Correct ✓Structure
I want to speak, hear and writing English. I want to speak, hear and write English. V1 + and + V1
He is handsome, clever and slowly. He is handsome, clever and smart/loyal/talented. adj + and + adj
I like singing, dancing and to walk. I like singing, dancing and walking. V-ing + and + V-ing
He asked her to cook, to rest and prepare the dish. He asked her to cook, to rest and to prepare the dish. to+V + and + to+V
My friends never judge me by my words or what I did. My friends never judge me by my words or my actions. noun + or + noun
Tom is known for his honesty and original ideas. Tom is known for his honesty and originality. noun + and + noun (better)
To learn is understanding the world. To learn is to understand the world. to+V + is + to+V (better)
I like to read books over watching movies. I like reading books over watching movies. V-ing + over + V-ing
I would rather pay than financial aid. I would rather pay than receive financial aid. V1 + than + V1
This task can be done individually, pairs or groups of four. This task can be done individually, in pairs or in groups of four. in+noun + or + in+noun
not only…but also → must be parallel
✗
The teacher not only was his students wants…
not only…but also are not placed at parallel positions. ✗
✓
The teacher wants students not only to keep quiet but also to do the task.
not only [to+V] but also [to+V] → parallel. ✓
💡
How to spot a Parallelism error:
1. Find the connector (and / or / not only…but also / neither…nor / either…or / rather…than)
2. Look at the element BEFORE the connector → identify its part of speech / form
3. The element AFTER the connector must be the EXACT same form
4. If they don't match → that's the error of parallelism!

"singing, dancing and to walk" → -ing ≠ to+V → fix: "singing, dancing and walking" ✓

📋 Rule 80 — Quick Recap

  • Parallelism = both sides of a connector must use the same part of speech / same form
  • Connectors requiring parallel structure: and · or · not only…but also · either…or · neither…nor · rather…than
  • V1 + and + V1 ✓: "speak, hear and write" · adj + and + adj ✓: "handsome, clever and smart"
  • V-ing + and + V-ing ✓: "singing, dancing and walking" · to+V + and + to+V ✓: "to cook, to rest and to prepare"
  • noun + and + noun ✓: "honesty and originality" · V1 + rather than + V1 ✓: "pay rather than receive"

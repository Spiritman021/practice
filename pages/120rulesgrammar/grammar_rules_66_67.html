"male/female" vs "man/woman" ‚Äî Characteristics vs Profession/Gender

male/female = describes the USE or TARGET GROUP of the noun ¬∑ man/woman = describes the GENDER of the person in that profession

‚Üí The Core Rule
‚òÖ From the book (Rule 67):
Whenever "male" or "female" is used before a noun, it means that we are describing its characteristics/use ‚Äî i.e. what the noun is designed for or used by.

But whenever we use "man" or "woman" before a noun, it means that we are describing the profession or post of that noun ‚Äî i.e. the gender of the person.
male / female
= Describes CHARACTERISTICS / USE / TARGET GROUP
female watch= watches designed/specified for females
female doctor= doctor who treats females (gynecologist)
male shoes= shoes designed for males
male belt= belt meant for males
female purse= purse designed for females
man / woman
= Describes GENDER of the person in that profession
woman doctor= the doctor herself is a woman
man soldier= the soldier himself is a man
woman student= the student herself is a woman
man doctor= the doctor himself is a man
woman teacher= the teacher herself is a woman

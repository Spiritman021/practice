63

Omission of Article "The" — 10 Cases Where "The" is NOT Used

Cities · Countries (non-abbreviated) · Continents · Streets · Singular lakes/bays · Mountain peaks · Single islands · Languages · Sports · General words (season/meals/God/life…)

→ All 10 Categories — Overview
★ From the book (Rule 63): Omission of Article "The": We do not use any article before the following nouns:
(1)
Names of Cities
Los Angeles, Alaska, Sydney, New Delhi, Alwar, etc.
(2)
Names of Countries
(except abbreviated) Australia, Bolivia, England, France, Spain, India, Japan, China, etc.
(3)
Names of Continents
Asia, Europe, Australia, Africa, South America, North America, Antarctica.
(4)
Names of Streets
George Street, Albion Street, New Town Street, etc.
(5)
Singular Lakes & Bays
Lake Carey, Lake Eyre, Lake Hillier, Shark Bay, etc.
(6)
Mountain Peaks ⭐
Mount Everest, Mount Solitary, Mount Bindo, Mount Fuji, etc. (NOT "the" Mount Everest)
(7)
Single Islands
Easter Island, Bare Island, Bird Island, Fatima Island, etc.
(8)
Languages
Spanish, Russian, English. (Note: "the English" = people of England)
(9)
Sports
Cricket, football, basketball, hockey, etc.
(10)
General Words
season, language, drinks, meals, God, life, money, etc. (BUT: if particularized → use "the")
★ Detailed Explanations & Examples
(1) Cities — never "The"
✗
The New Delhi is the capital of India. [Incorrect]
City names → never use "the". ✗
✓
New Delhi is the capital of India. [Correct]
No article before city names. ✓
(8) Languages — special note
✓
I do not know English. [Correct — language name]
No "the" before language names. ✓
✓
The English are very disciplined. [Correct — "the English" = people of England]
"The" before language name → refers to the PEOPLE of that country/language group. ✓
Note: "the English" = people of England · "the Hindi" = people of Hindi-speaking countries · This is a meaning-change, not an error.
(10) General words — "the" only when particularized
★ From the book: In normal cases, we do not use any article before these words — season, language, drinks, meals, God, life, money, etc. However, when we use these words to particularize the information, we use the article "the".
✓
Breakfast should be light. [Correct — general statement, no "the"]
No specific breakfast referred to → no "the". ✓
✓
But the breakfast I had this morning was heavy. [Correct — particularized]
"the breakfast" = that specific breakfast → "the" used. ✓
✓
God is everywhere. [Correct — no "the" in general sense]
✓
But the God that you worship is kind. [Correct — particularized]
Specific God pointed out → "the". ✓
✓
I don't know English. [Correct — general] BUT: The English that you speak is very clear. [Correct — particularized]
💡
Key concept for (10):
If you are making a general statement → no "the".
If you point out / particularize that specific thing → "the" is needed.
Think: "Are you talking about one specific [breakfast/God/English]? → Yes → use 'the'."

📋 Rule 63 — Quick Recap (10 "No The" Categories)

  • (1) Cities: New Delhi, Sydney, Mumbai → NO "the"
  • (2) Countries (non-abbreviated): India, France, Japan → NO "the" · (USA, UK, UAE → use "the")
  • (3) Continents: Asia, Europe, Africa → NO "the"
  • (4) Streets: George Street → NO "the"
  • (5) Singular lakes/bays: Lake Eyre, Shark Bay → NO "the"
  • (6) Mountain peaks: Mount Everest, Mount Fuji → NO "the" ⭐ (but mountain ranges: The Himalayas ✓)
  • (7) Single islands: Easter Island → NO "the"
  • (8) Languages: English, Spanish → NO "the" · (Exception: "the English" = people of England)
  • (9) Sports: cricket, football → NO "the"
  • (10) General words: season/meals/God/life → NO "the" in general · BUT: if particularized → use "the"
64

"The" + Adjective = Entire Community · Plural Noun Meaning · Plural Verb

Whenever "the" is placed before an adjective (without a noun), it represents the whole group/community of that type of people

→ The Core Rule
★ From the book (Rule 64): Use of "the + Adjective": Whenever the article "the" is used before an adjective, it determines the whole group.

['The' + 'adjective'] = Plural noun — refers to the entire community of people with that quality.
the + adjective (no noun after) = all people with that quality → always treated as plural noun → plural verb
The poor
= all poor people
poor people
The needy
= all needy people
needy people
The brave
= all brave people
brave people
The rich
= all rich people
rich people
The honest
= all honest people
honest people
The blind
= all blind people
blind people
★ All Examples from Book & Class
✓
The poor are suffering. [Correct]
"The poor" = poor people (plural) → plural verb "are". ✓
✓
The honest are respected. [Correct]
"The honest" = honest people → plural verb "are". ✓ ['The' + 'adjective' = Plural noun] It means honest people
✓
The poor are not always dishonest. [Correct]
"The poor" = poor people → plural verb. ✓
✓
The needy require our help. [Correct]
"The needy" = needy people (plural) → plural verb. ✓
✓
The brave deserve medals. [Correct]
"The brave" = brave people (plural) → plural verb. ✓
✓
The rich have many privileges. [Correct]
"The rich" = rich people (plural) → plural verb "have". ✓
💡
Memory Trick:
"the + adjective" secretly has "people" hiding after the adjective.
"The poor" = "The poor [people]" → plural → plural verb.
Always test: "Can I add 'people' after the adjective?" → If yes → it's this rule!

📋 Rule 64 — Quick Recap

  • the + adjective (no noun) = entire community: "the poor" = poor people · "the brave" = brave people
  • Always treated as plural noun → always use plural verb: "The poor are suffering." ✓
  • Examples: the poor · the needy · the brave · the rich · the honest · the blind
65

Same Nouns Connected by a Preposition → Always Singular Form & Singular Verb

When two identical nouns are linked by a preposition (after, to, by…) → both nouns must be in singular form · verb is also singular

→ The Core Rule
★ From the book (Rule 65): Whenever same nouns are connected by a preposition, in that case we use both the nouns in a singular form.
Noun + preposition + same Noun → both nouns = SINGULAR · Verb = SINGULAR
"village after village" ✓ (NOT "villages after villages") · verb = "was" (not "were")
★ All Examples from Book & Class
village after village
✗
Villages after villages were destroyed by the terrorists. [Incorrect]
Both nouns must be singular. Verb "were" also wrong (should be "was"). ✗
✓
Village after village was destroyed by the terrorists. [Correct]
Both "village" → singular. Verb "was" → singular. ✓
book after book
✗
Books after books had been read by him. [Incorrect]
Both nouns must be singular. ✗
✓
Book after book had been read by him. [Correct]
Both "book" → singular. ✓
Other correct examples from book
✓
City after city was set on fire. [Correct]
Both "city" → singular. Verb "was" → singular. ✓
✓
Page after page was reviewed multiple times. [Correct]
Both "page" → singular. ✓
✓
Door to door. [Correct]
"door" + "to" + "door" → both singular. ✓
✓
Step by step. [Correct]
"step" + "by" + "step" → both singular. ✓
✓
Face to face. [Correct]
"face" + "to" + "face" → both singular. ✓
💡
Two rules together:
(1) Both nouns → singular form (never plural)
(2) Verb → singular (because the subject is treated as one unit): "Village after village was destroyed." ✓ · "were" ✗

📋 Rule 65 — Quick Recap

  • Same noun + preposition + same noun → both nouns in singular. "village after village" ✓ · "villages after villages" ✗
  • Verb is also singular: "Village after village was destroyed." ✓ · "were" ✗
  • All correct forms: "book after book" · "city after city" · "page after page" · "door to door" · "step by step" · "face to face"

Core Rule:"the" + adjective (no noun) = entire community of people with that quality → treated as plural noun → plural verb.
Examples:"The poor" = poor people · "The needy" = needy people · "The brave" = brave people · "The rich" = rich people · "The honest" = honest people

Rule 65 — Same Noun + Preposition → Singular Noun Form
Core Rule:Same noun connected by a preposition → BOTH nouns in singular form · Verb also singular.
Wrong → Right:"Villages after villages were destroyed." ✗ → "Village after village was destroyed." ✓ · "Books after books…" ✗ → "Book after book…" ✓
All correct forms:"village after village" · "book after book" · "city after city" · "page after page" · "door to door" · "step by step" · "face to face"
Verb note:"Village after village was destroyed." (singular verb — not "were") ✓

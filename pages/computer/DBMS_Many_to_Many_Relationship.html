Meaning of Many-to-Many

In a many-to-many relationship, many instances on one side can be associated with many instances on the other side. A student can study multiple courses, and a course can be studied by multiple students.

Study Relationship Table

The relationship table stores which student studies which course. It must contain Roll_No and CID. Both are Foreign Keys referencing their base tables.

Study Relationship Table

2C1Student 2 studies DBMS
2C2Student 2 studies OS
Roll_No repeats because one student can study many courses. CID repeats because one course can be studied by many students.
Primary key of Study: Neither Roll_No alone nor CID alone can be Primary Key because both repeat. The Primary Key is the composite key (Roll_No, CID).

Can Tables Be Reduced?

No. In a many-to-many relationship, table reduction is not possible. The relationship table must remain separate because its Primary Key is a combination of both participating entity keys.

No Reduction in M:N
Try merging with Student?
Cannot merge, because Roll_No repeats in Study while it is unique in Student.
✕
Try merging with Course?
Cannot merge, because CID repeats in Study while it is unique in Course.
Final number of tables remains 3: Student, Course, and Study.
Exam takeaway: In many-to-many, relationship table has a composite Primary Key made from both Foreign Keys, and tables cannot be reduced.

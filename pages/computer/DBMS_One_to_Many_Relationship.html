Meaning of One-to-Many

In a one-to-many relationship, one entity instance on the first side can be associated with many instances on the second side. In the classic example, one Customer can give many Orders, but one particular order belongs to only one customer.

Relationship Table

The relationship table tells which customer gave which order. It must contain the Primary Keys of both participating entities as Foreign Keys. It may also contain its own descriptive attribute, such as Date.

Customer ID can repeat because one customer can give many orders. Order_No does not repeat because each order belongs to one customer.
Primary key of relationship table: In 1:N, the Primary Key of the many side becomes the Primary Key of the relationship table. Here, Order_No is the Primary Key of Gives.
4

Table Reduction

A one-to-many relationship can be reduced from three tables to two by merging the relationship table with the table on the many side. Here, merge Gives with Order, not with Customer.

Merge on the Many Side
Before Reduction

After Reduction
Merge Gives with Order because Order is the many side. Final result = 2 tables.

Core Rule
Relationship:Customer gives Order.
Cardinality:One customer can give many orders; one order belongs to one customer.
Notation:1:N or 1:M.
2
Relationship Table
Mandatory columns:ID and Order_No.
Foreign keys:ID references Customer(ID), Order_No references Order(Order_No).
Descriptive attr:Date can be added to the relationship table.
Repeating side:Customer ID can repeat; Order_No cannot repeat.

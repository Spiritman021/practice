Boyce-Codd Normal Form
BCNF in DBMS

BCNF is a stricter special case of 3NF. For every functional dependency, the left-hand side must be a candidate key or super key.

1

What is BCNF?

BCNF stands for Boyce-Codd Normal Form. It is called a special case of Third Normal Form because it is stricter than 3NF.

BCNF condition: for every functional dependency X → Y, the left-hand side X must be a candidate key or super key.
2

3NF vs BCNF

3NF Condition
For every FD X → Y, either X is a candidate key/super key OR Y is a prime attribute.
BCNF Condition
The OR option is removed. For every FD, X itself must be a candidate key or super key.
So every BCNF table is in 3NF, but every 3NF table is not necessarily in BCNF.
3

Student Table Example

Consider a student table with attributes RollNo, Name, VoterID, Age. Candidate keys are RollNo and VoterID.

Normal Form Hierarchy

BCNF is the innermost and most restrictive among the forms discussed here. Being in a lower normal form does not automatically mean being in a higher one.

1NF → 2NF → 3NF → BCNF

If a table is in 2NF, it may or may not be in 3NF. If a table is in 3NF, it may or may not be in BCNF. You must check the next condition separately.

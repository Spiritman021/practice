What is a Weak Entity?

A Weak Entity is an entity that does not have its own Primary Key. It cannot be uniquely identified by its own attributes alone, so it depends on another entity called the Owner Entity.

Main rule: Weak entities do not have a proper Primary Key and cannot exist independently.

Employee-Dependent Example

A company stores employee data using Emp_ID as Primary Key. Dependents of employees, such as spouse or children, are known through the employee. The company does not identify dependents independently; it identifies them through the owner employee.

Why Partial Key?

A weak entity has no complete Primary Key. So we choose a partial key, also called discriminator, that helps distinguish weak entity records under the same owner. In the example, Dep_Name can be a partial key, even though names may repeat across different employees.

Conversion to Relational Tables

While converting to relational tables, do not create three separate tables for Employee, Has, and Dependent. The weak entity and identifying relationship are merged. Final result is two tables: Employee and Has_Dependent.

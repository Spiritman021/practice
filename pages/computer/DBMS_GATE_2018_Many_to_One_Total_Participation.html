Question Statement

GATE 2018 — Relationship Model

Consider two entity sets E1 and E2 connected by a relationship R. The relationship is many-to-one from E1 to E2, and both entity sets participate totally in the relationship. Which statement is true?

A. Every entity in E1 is associated with exactly one entity in E2.
B. Some entity in E1 is associated with more than one entity in E2.
C. Every entity in E2 is associated with exactly one entity in E1.
D. Every entity in E2 is associated with at most one entity in E1.
2

Draw the Relationship

A many-to-one relationship from E1 to E2 means many entities of E1 can map to one entity of E2. But one entity of E1 cannot map to multiple entities of E2.

Why It Works

Every E1 entity appears exactly once. Every E2 entity appears at least once. Multiple E1 entities may point to the same E2 entity, so the relationship remains many-to-one.

This is like each student studying in exactly one college, while a college can have many students.
4

Option Analysis

A is True
Total participation of E1 means every E1 must participate. Many-to-one means each E1 can connect to only one E2. So every E1 is associated with exactly one E2.
B is False
If one E1 entity connects to more than one E2 entity, it becomes many-to-many from the E1 perspective. That violates many-to-one.
C is False
An E2 entity can be associated with many E1 entities. It is not restricted to exactly one E1.
D is False
At most one E1 for each E2 would mean one-to-one, not many-to-one. In M:1, an E2 can have multiple E1 entities.
Shortcut: M:1 from E1 to E2 + total participation of E1 = every E1 has exactly one E2.
Final Answer: Option A — Every entity in E1 is associated with exactly one entity in E2.

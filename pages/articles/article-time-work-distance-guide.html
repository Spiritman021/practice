Time, Work, and Distance Quant Guide

By Practice Hub Editorial Team · Updated May 16, 2026

These topics become easy when you convert wordy statements into rate equations quickly. Most students lose marks because setup is slow, not because concept is hard.

Time and Work

Use efficiency units and LCM approach for multi-person work questions. Track who joins or leaves and split total work into timeline blocks.

Pipes and Cistern

Treat filling as positive rate and emptying as negative rate. Keep signs clean and convert final equation into one combined rate.

Time Speed Distance

Memorize relation triangle and standard train/platform formulas. Relative speed logic should become reflex for opposite and same-direction movement.

Practice System

Practice 15-question mixed sets with 20-minute cap. First target is clear setup in under 25 seconds per question.

Rate Conversion Foundation

For both time-work and time-distance, rate conversion is the core skill. If rate setup is correct, solving becomes straightforward. Train yourself to convert statements into “work per day” or “distance per hour” form quickly before touching options.

Time and Work Advanced Patterns

Common government exam variations include alternate day work, efficiency comparisons, partial work completion, and worker replacement. The fastest method is to use total work units and timeline segmentation. Avoid direct fraction mixing without a clear unit base.

Train and Relative Speed Questions

In time-distance, train questions are high scoring when formula memory is strong. Practice opposite and same-direction relative speed separately. Also train conversion between km/hr and m/s until it becomes reflex-level.

Boats and Streams Logic

Downstream speed is sum and upstream speed is difference. Keep this relation mentally fixed and avoid sign confusion. Many students know the concept but lose marks due to rushed substitution.

Question Selection Under Timer

Attempt direct setup questions first and leave multi-condition cases for second pass. If equation setup exceeds 30 seconds and demand is unclear, mark and move. This saves time for easier marks in the same section.

Weekly Drill Plan

Practice these topics on alternate days with small mixed sets. End each session by reviewing one wrong question deeply and rewriting the ideal setup. Rewriting correct setup is the fastest way to build long-term speed and confidence.

Final Exam Execution Tip

In live tests, avoid emotional solving after one difficult question. Reset quickly, solve one easy item to regain momentum, and then return to moderate questions. Controlled flow is the key to strong time-work-distance scoring.

Last 10-Day Strategy

In final revision window, focus on mixed timed sets and correction notebook review. Avoid collecting too many new tricks and prioritize stable execution of known methods.

Confidence Stabilization

End each session with one correctly solved moderate question to reinforce positive momentum before the next study block.

Field Note from Practice

These chapters become easier once setup speed improves; most delays come from equation formation, not from the final arithmetic.

Editor's Closing Advice

Practice under mixed conditions so you can switch quickly between work-rate and distance-rate logic in one paper.

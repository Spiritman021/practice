7-Day Revision Cycle for Quant

By Practice Hub Editorial Team · Updated May 16, 2026

This weekly revision loop is designed for government exam aspirants who want steady quant improvement without mental overload. Instead of random topic jumping, this structure keeps your memory fresh, speed stable, and confidence high.

Day 1: Table Recall and Reverse Logic

Revise tables, reverse products, and quick division mapping. Keep this fast and timed. Goal is reflex-level recall, not written solving.

Day 2: Arithmetic Speed Drill

Practice mixed multiplication patterns and short simplification sets. Track time per question and error type.

Day 3: Squares and Cubes

Memorize and test squares to 30 and cubes to 20. Use both direct and reverse question formats.

Day 4: Product Pattern Consolidation

Use consecutive products and factor recognition practice. This improves approximation and mental math for DI and arithmetic.

Day 5: Timed Mini Mock

Attempt one strict timed set. Focus on selection quality, not maximum attempts. Keep a negative-marking safe approach.

Day 6: Weak-Area Repair

Review your wrong notebook and re-solve repeated patterns. Correct method and speed issues separately.

Day 7: Full Simulation and Review

Take a full practice test and do complete analysis. End day with a short summary: three strengths, three corrections for next week.

How to Repeat

Run this cycle for 4 to 6 weeks. Maintain consistency and compare weekly trends in accuracy and time. This method builds long-term exam readiness.

Session Quality Rules

Each revision session should have a measurable finish condition. Example: complete 25 table conversions with 90 percent accuracy, or solve 15 mixed arithmetic questions in under 12 minutes. Clear finish rules make revision objective and prevent passive study.

How to Use Error Notebook in This Cycle

On Day 6 and Day 7, your correction notebook becomes central. Sort errors into recurring and one-time. Recurring errors need repeated drills and trigger reminders. One-time errors only need brief review. This prioritization saves time and improves recovery quality.

Memory Reinforcement Trick

At the end of each day, do a 3-minute quick recall test without looking at notes. Speak key formulas or conversion rules aloud. Verbal recall improves retention and exposes weak areas quickly.

When to Increase Difficulty

Increase speed pressure only after hitting your current target for three consecutive sessions. If consistency is not there, keep the same level and improve control first. Premature difficulty jumps often damage confidence and raise careless mistakes.

Mini Reset Between Days

Before starting each day’s session, use a short focus reset like breathing or scan drill. Even 2 to 3 minutes helps reduce distraction carryover from work or other subjects.

Expected Outcome After 4 Weeks

With steady execution, you should see faster arithmetic recall, reduced repeated errors, and improved mock stability. The biggest win is not only higher score, but less score fluctuation from test to test.

Field Note from Practice

A weekly loop with correction day built in helps students retain speed while reducing repeat mistakes across chapters.

Editor's Closing Advice

At the end of each week, keep a 3-line summary: what improved, what stayed weak, and what next week should prioritize.

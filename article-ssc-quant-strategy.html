SSC Quant Preparation Strategy

SSC CGL and CHSL quant preparation works best when you build a repeatable system, not random practice. Most aspirants know formulas but lose marks because calculation speed and attempt discipline are weak. The objective should be simple: improve direct question hit-rate, reduce silly errors, and keep mental stamina stable for full test duration.

Phase 1: Foundation to Speed

Start with tables 1 to 30, squares to 30, cubes to 20, and reverse product recall. This is not basic work, it is your scoring engine. Spend 15 to 20 minutes daily on quick drills. Use table practice tools until you can answer direct products and divisions in under 3 seconds. This single habit saves major time in simplification, percentage, ratio, and DI calculations.

Phase 2: Timed Arithmetic Sets

Move to short timed sets of 20 to 25 questions. Practice two rounds: first for accuracy, second for speed. Use skip-first logic: if a question feels calculation-heavy in first view, leave it and return later. Do not spend more than 40 to 50 seconds on one uncertain item in round one.

Phase 3: Mock and Error Journal

After each mock, divide wrong answers into concept error, speed error, and careless error. This classification is critical. Concept errors need topic revision. Speed errors need drill repetition. Careless errors need focus routine and slower final checking. Keep one correction notebook and revise that before every mock.

Weekly Execution Pattern

Follow this cycle: 4 days speed drills, 2 days mock simulation, 1 day deep review. If consistency is maintained for 6 to 8 weeks, both confidence and score stability improve significantly.

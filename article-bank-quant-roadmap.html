Bank Exam Quantitative Aptitude Roadmap

Banking quant is not only about solving tough questions; it is about solving the right questions quickly with high accuracy. In IBPS and SBI level exams, time pressure and negative marking reward smart selection more than blind attempts. Your preparation should combine speed math, mock discipline, and clear review rules.

Daily Core Block

Keep a 25-minute arithmetic speed routine: multiplication recall, reverse tables, squares, cubes, and percentage conversions. These improve DI handling, approximation, and simplification. If your fundamentals are automatic, your brain stays free for question selection and logic under pressure.

Sectional Mock Framework

Attempt short sectional mocks 4 to 5 times per week. Use two-step tracking: attempt rate and accuracy rate. A good target is 80 to 90 percent accuracy before increasing attempts aggressively. If attempts are high but accuracy falls below 75 percent, reduce risk and rebuild control.

Attempt Order Strategy

In real tests, start with clean direct questions and quick arithmetic. Move to moderate questions after confidence builds. Leave calculation traps and doubtful items for final pass. This protects marks and avoids early panic cycles caused by one stubborn question.

Review and Recovery Routine

After each mock, list top five mistakes and rewrite corrected approach in one line each. Revise this page daily before practice. Add a 5-minute breathing or focus task before every mock to reduce rushed clicks and improve decision quality.

Weekly Plan

Use this split: 4 days speed drills plus sectional tests, 2 days full mock simulation, 1 day deep analysis. This balanced cycle improves both score and consistency.

Daily Study Plan for Government Job Preparation

Most students fail not because of low effort, but because daily practice is unstructured. A practical routine that you can repeat for months is more powerful than a perfect plan followed for one week. This timetable is designed for working aspirants and college students preparing alongside other commitments.

Standard 45-Minute Plan

Block 1 (15 min): table recall and reverse table drills. Block 2 (15 min): speed arithmetic with squares, cubes, and product patterns. Block 3 (15 min): timed mock or sectional quiz with quick review. This gives balanced development across memory, speed, and exam execution.

20-Minute Backup Plan

On busy days, do not skip completely. Use a short version: 8 minutes table drill, 8 minutes mini mock, 4 minutes correction review. This keeps continuity alive and prevents restart fatigue after gaps.

Weekly Structure

Follow a 5 plus 2 model. Five days for core practice and two days for full mock plus deep analysis. On review days, focus on repeated error patterns from your notebook, not random new questions.

Progress Tracking

Track three numbers daily: accuracy, time per set, and error type. Weekly targets should be realistic, for example improving accuracy from 76 percent to 82 percent in two weeks.

Mindset Rule

Consistency beats intensity. Avoid all-or-nothing thinking. Even small daily sessions compound into strong exam readiness over time.
